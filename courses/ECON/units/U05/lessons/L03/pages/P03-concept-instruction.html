Concept Instruction

Credit is the ability to borrow money now and pay it back later. Credit can be useful when a person needs to make a purchase before having enough cash available.

When someone borrows money, that person takes on debt. Debt is money owed to a lender and must be repaid according to the agreement.

A loan is a common form of credit. Loans are often used for major purchases such as a car, education, or other large expenses.

Lenders usually charge interest for allowing someone to borrow money. The interest rate is the percentage charged on the loan. A higher interest rate means the borrower will pay more over time.

Credit can provide flexibility, but it also creates financial responsibility. Borrowers must make payments on time and should understand how much the loan will cost in total.

Responsible use of credit means borrowing only what can reasonably be repaid. If a person borrows too much or ignores payment deadlines, debt can become difficult to manage.

Good financial decisions involve comparing the need to borrow with the cost of borrowing. This helps people avoid unnecessary debt and protect long-term financial stability.

🧠Think: Why might two people borrowing the same amount of money end up paying different total amounts over time?

⚠️Common mistake: Focusing only on the amount borrowed and ignoring the interest rate. The cost of interest can make a loan much more expensive than the original amount borrowed.
