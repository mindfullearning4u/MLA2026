Worked Example

💡 Read the sample scenario and observe how a consumer can evaluate financial risk before making a purchase.

Scenario: A student wants headphones and is deciding between a very cheap pair with poor reviews and a moderately priced pair with better quality and a warranty.

Decision-making strategy: The student compares cost, quality, durability, and long-term value.

Financial risk: The cheaper pair may break quickly, creating the risk of paying again for a replacement.

Smarter choice: The better-quality pair may cost more now but may reduce risk and provide better long-term value.

Financial reasoning: Good consumer decisions compare more than price alone.

Why this works:

  • It shows how evaluating purchases involves more than choosing the lowest price.
  • It explains financial risk in a simple way.
  • It connects decision-making to long-term value.
