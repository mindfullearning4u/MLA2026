Worked Example

💡 Read the sample scenario and observe how multiple Unit 05 concepts are used together.

Scenario: A student earns part-time income, wants to save for a car, needs to pay monthly phone costs, and is considering using credit to buy a laptop.

Budgeting: The student should compare income with monthly expenses and decide how much money can go to needs, saving, and optional spending.

Saving goal: Setting aside money regularly can help the student work toward the car purchase.

Credit decision: Using credit for the laptop could help now, but interest would increase the total cost.

Consumer decision-making: The student should compare laptop options and decide whether borrowing is necessary or whether saving first would be more responsible.

Conclusion: Good personal finance choices require balancing current needs, future goals, and the cost of borrowing.

Why this works:

  • It connects budgeting, saving, credit, and consumer choice in one scenario.
  • It shows how one decision can affect several parts of a financial plan.
  • It uses direct financial reasoning rather than isolated definitions.
