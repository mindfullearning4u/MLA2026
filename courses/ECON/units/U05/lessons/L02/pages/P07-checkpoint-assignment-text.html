Lesson 2 Checkpoint - Saving and Investing Notebook Evidence

This checkpoint verifies your ability to distinguish saving from investing and explain how each can support financial security.

You must choose ONE of the three scenarios from the guided practice:
- A student puts money into a savings account for emergency expenses that might happen soon.
- A worker puts money into a long-term investment account for future financial growth.
- A family splits money between short-term savings and a long-term investment goal.

Required evidence:
- The scenario you selected (must match one of the three options).
- Whether the main choice is saving, investing, or both.
- An explanation of how interest or return could help the money grow.
- An explanation of the level of risk in the scenario.
- One sentence explaining why the choice supports financial security.

Accepted submission formats:
- jpg
- png
- pdf

Your Teacher of Record will review this submission for completion and accuracy.

If the scenario is not one of the three provided options, or if saving, investing, growth, and risk are unclear, the checkpoint is not complete.

This checkpoint contributes to unit mastery and progression.
