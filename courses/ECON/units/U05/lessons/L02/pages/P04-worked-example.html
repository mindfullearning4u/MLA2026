Worked Example

💡 Read the sample scenario and observe how saving and investing serve different financial goals.

Scenario: A student wants to keep some money available for emergencies but also wants money to grow for a goal that is several years away.

Saving choice: The student puts part of the money in a savings account for easy access and lower risk.

Investing choice: The student puts another part into a long-term investment to try to earn a greater return.

Interest and growth: The savings account may earn interest, while the investment may grow more over time but with more risk.

Financial reasoning: Using both options helps balance short-term security with long-term financial growth.

Why this works:

  • It shows the difference between saving and investing clearly.
  • It explains the role of interest and long-term growth.
  • It connects financial choices to different goals and levels of risk.
