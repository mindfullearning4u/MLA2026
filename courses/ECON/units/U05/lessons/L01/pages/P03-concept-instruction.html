Concept | Income and Budgeting

Income is the money a person earns from work or other sources. Income gives people the ability to pay for expenses, save money, and plan for future financial needs.

A budget is a plan for how money will be used. A budget helps a person compare income to spending so money can be managed responsibly.

One important part of budgeting is understanding the difference between needs and wants. Needs are things a person must have, such as food, housing, and basic transportation. Wants are things a person may enjoy but does not need for basic living, such as entertainment or luxury items.

Another important skill is expense tracking. Expense tracking means writing down or recording how money is spent. This helps people see where their money is going and decide whether they are staying within their budget.

Budgeting is part of financial planning. Financial planning means making choices now that help meet short-term and long-term money goals. A person who plans carefully is more likely to avoid overspending and be prepared for future expenses.

Responsible budgeting does not mean never spending money on wants. It means making sure needs are covered first and using income in a way that matches personal priorities and goals.

🧠 Think: If your income for the month is limited, how would you decide which expenses should come first?

⚠️ Common mistake: Assuming a budget is only for people with a lot of money. Anyone who earns and spends money can benefit from having a budget.
