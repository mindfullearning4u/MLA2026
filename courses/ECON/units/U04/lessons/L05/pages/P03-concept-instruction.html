Unit 04 | Lesson 5

Concept Instruction

In this lesson, you are not learning a new concept. Instead, you are combining all concepts from Unit 04 to analyze how government policies affect the economy.

Strong policy analysis begins by asking why the government is acting. A policy may exist to provide a public good, respond to a market failure, raise revenue, encourage economic activity, or protect the public through regulation.

A complete analysis should also identify how the government is acting. Is it collecting taxes, increasing spending, changing incentives through fiscal policy, or creating rules through regulation?

Good economic reasoning explains both benefits and tradeoffs. A policy may solve a public problem, but it may also increase costs, change behavior, or affect business decisions.

To analyze any policy scenario, you should ask:

  • What public need or economic problem is being addressed?
  • What role is government playing?
  • Does the policy involve taxes, spending, or regulation?
  • What public benefits could result?
  • What costs or tradeoffs could result?

The strongest response does not just list concepts separately. It explains how those concepts work together in one policy decision.

🧠Think: Why might a policy that helps the public in one way still create economic costs somewhere else?

⚠️Common mistake: Explaining only the positive or only the negative side of a policy. Strong analysis includes both intended benefits and likely tradeoffs.
