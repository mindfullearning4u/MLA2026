Unit 04 Lesson 1 - Role of Government in the Economy

📘 Write these required notes in your notebook:

  • Public good: a good or service that people can use without reducing another person's use, such as streetlights or national defense.
  • Government intervention: action taken by government to influence the economy when markets do not produce the desired outcome on their own.
  • Regulation: rules created by government to guide business activity and protect the public.
  • Market failure: a situation in which the market does not distribute resources efficiently or fairly.
  • Why government acts: governments may provide public goods, reduce harm, protect consumers, and respond when markets fail.
  • Key idea: government has an economic role because markets do not always meet every public need by themselves.
