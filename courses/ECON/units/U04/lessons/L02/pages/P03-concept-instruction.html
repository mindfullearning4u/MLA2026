Concept Instruction

Governments need revenue to provide services and carry out their responsibilities. One major way governments raise revenue is by collecting taxes.

An income tax is collected from money earned by workers or businesses. If a person receives wages from a job, part of that income may be paid in taxes.

A sales tax is added when people buy many goods or services. For example, if a shirt costs $20, the final price may be higher after sales tax is added.

A property tax is based on the value of property such as homes, land, or buildings. Property taxes often help support local government services such as schools, roads, and emergency services.

Taxes serve an important purpose in the economy. They help governments pay for services such as public education, transportation, law enforcement, and parks.

Taxes also affect decision-making. Individuals may notice taxes when earning income or buying products. Businesses may consider taxes when setting prices, planning investments, or deciding where to operate.

Taxes can support public needs, but they also create costs for households and businesses. That is why tax systems are an important part of economic policy.

🧠Think: Which type of tax are you most likely to notice first as a consumer buying everyday items?

⚠️Common mistake: Assuming all taxes work the same way. Different taxes are collected in different ways and affect people and businesses differently.
