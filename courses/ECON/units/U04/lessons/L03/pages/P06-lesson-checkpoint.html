🎯 Lesson Checkpoint | Fiscal Policy Notebook Evidence
This checkpoint verifies your ability to identify fiscal policy, classify it correctly, and explain how it is intended to affect the economy.

Important: You must choose ONE of the three scenarios from the guided practice:

  • The government increases spending on school construction during an economic slowdown.
  • The government raises taxes and cuts spending because leaders want to slow overall economic activity.
  • The government lowers taxes for households in order to encourage more spending.

Checkpoint Submission Requirements (Required):

  • The scenario you selected (must match one of the three options).
  • Whether the policy is expansionary or contractionary.
  • An explanation of what the government is doing with spending or taxes.
  • An explanation of how the policy is intended to influence the economy.
  • One sentence explaining why that policy makes economic sense in the scenario.

If the scenario is not one of the three provided options, or if the fiscal policy and economic reasoning are not clearly identified, the checkpoint is not complete.
