Unit 04 Lesson 4 - Regulation and Economic Impact

📘 Write these required notes in your notebook:

  • Regulation: rules created by government to guide business behavior and economic activity.
  • Consumer protection: rules that help protect buyers from unsafe, misleading, or unfair business practices.
  • Environmental protection: rules designed to reduce harm to land, air, water, and public health.
  • Economic tradeoff: a benefit gained in one area that may create a cost in another area.
  • Balancing regulation and freedom: governments try to protect the public while still allowing businesses to operate and compete.
  • Key idea: regulation can create public benefits, but it can also increase costs or limit some business choices.
