Lesson 4 Checkpoint - Regulation Notebook Evidence

This checkpoint verifies your ability to explain the purpose of a regulation and analyze its economic tradeoffs.

You must choose ONE of the three scenarios from the guided practice:
- A government requires food companies to list ingredients and nutrition information on packages.
- A city limits pollution from factories located near neighborhoods and schools.
- A new safety rule requires toy companies to test products more carefully before selling them.

Required evidence:
- The scenario you selected (must match one of the three options).
- The main purpose of the regulation.
- An explanation of one public benefit created by the regulation.
- An explanation of one economic cost or tradeoff the regulation may create.
- One sentence explaining whether the regulation seems economically reasonable and why.

Accepted submission formats:
- jpg
- png
- pdf

Your Teacher of Record will review this submission for completion and accuracy.

If the scenario is not one of the three provided options, or if the regulation purpose and tradeoff are unclear, the checkpoint is not complete.

This checkpoint contributes to unit mastery and progression.
