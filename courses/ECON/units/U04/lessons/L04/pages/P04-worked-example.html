Worked Example

💡 Read the sample scenario and observe how regulation can create both benefits and costs.

Scenario: The government requires factories to install equipment that reduces harmful air pollution.

Purpose of regulation: Environmental protection

Public benefit: Cleaner air can improve health and reduce environmental damage.

Business cost: Factories may need to spend more money to buy and maintain the required equipment.

Economic reasoning: The regulation creates a tradeoff because it protects the public but may increase business costs.

Why this works:

  • It identifies the purpose of the regulation clearly.
  • It explains a public benefit and a business cost.
  • It shows why regulation often involves economic tradeoffs.
