Worked Example

💡 Read the sample scenario and observe how trade-offs and opportunity cost are identified.

Scenario: A student has one free evening and must choose between studying for a test, working a shift, or attending a friend's birthday dinner.

Choice made: The student chooses to study for the test.

Trade-offs: The student gives up working the shift and attending the dinner.

Opportunity cost: If working the shift was the next best option, then the opportunity cost is the money and job experience the student gave up.

Why this works:

  • It identifies the decision clearly.
  • It separates all trade-offs from the next best alternative.
  • It shows that time and priorities affect economic choices.
