Concept | Opportunity Cost and Trade-Offs

Every time you make a choice, you face a trade-off. A trade-off includes all the options you give up when you choose one alternative over another.

The opportunity cost is more specific. It is the value of the next best alternative you did not choose. Economists focus on the next best alternative because it shows the real cost of a decision.

For example, if you choose to spend two hours studying instead of working, resting, or going out, your trade-off includes everything you gave up. If working was your next best option, then the opportunity cost of studying is the money or work experience you gave up.

🧠 Think: When you choose one activity after school, what is the next best thing you give up?

⚠️ Common mistake: Saying opportunity cost is every option not chosen. Opportunity cost is only the next best alternative.
