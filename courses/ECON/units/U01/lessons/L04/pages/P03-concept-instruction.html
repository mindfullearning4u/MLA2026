Concept | Incentives and Decision-Making

An incentive is something that encourages or discourages a decision. Incentives matter because people often compare benefits and costs before acting.

A positive incentive offers a reward. For example, a student may study harder to earn a high grade, a scholarship, or extra privileges.

A negative incentive adds a cost or consequence. For example, a late fee may discourage someone from paying a bill after the deadline, or a fine may discourage unsafe behavior.

In economics, incentives help explain why people change behavior. Businesses use incentives to attract customers and workers. Governments use incentives to influence public choices. Individuals respond to incentives when deciding how to spend time, money, and effort.

🧠 Think: What reward or consequence most strongly affects your decisions at school or at home?

⚠️ Common mistake: Assuming that incentives are always money. Incentives can also include time, grades, convenience, privileges, penalties, or risk.
