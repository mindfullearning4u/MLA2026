Worked Example

💡 Read the sample scenario and observe how economic thinking is applied.

Scenario: A student has $20 and wants to buy lunch, a movie ticket, and snacks after school.

Scarcity: The student does not have enough money to buy everything.

Need: Lunch is a need because food supports basic well-being.

Wants: The movie ticket and snacks are wants because they are optional.

Resource allocation: The student must decide how to use the limited $20 in the most reasonable way.

Why this works:

  • It recognizes that limited resources force choices.
  • It shows the difference between a need and a want.
  • It demonstrates how economics helps people prioritize decisions.
