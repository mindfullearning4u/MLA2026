💡Worked Example

💡Read the sample scenario and observe how multiple economic concepts are applied.

Scenario: A student must choose between getting a part-time job or joining an after-school sports team.

Scarcity: The student has limited time and cannot do both activities fully.

Opportunity cost: Choosing the job means giving up the sports experience; choosing sports means giving up income.

Incentives: The job offers money, while sports offer enjoyment, health, and social benefits.

Decision: The student must evaluate which option provides the greatest overall benefit.

✅Why this works:

  • It connects multiple economic concepts in one decision.
  • It clearly identifies scarcity, opportunity cost, and incentives.
  • It explains how the final choice depends on priorities.
