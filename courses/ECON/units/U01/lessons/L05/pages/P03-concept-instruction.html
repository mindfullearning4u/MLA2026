Concept | Applying Economic Thinking

In economics, real-world decisions are rarely based on one concept alone. Instead, individuals and organizations must consider scarcity, trade-offs, incentives, and the economic system in which they operate.

When analyzing a decision, you should ask:

  • What is limited? (scarcity)
  • What must be given up? (opportunity cost)
  • What incentives are influencing the decision?
  • Who is making the decision and why? (economic system)

Strong economic reasoning connects these ideas clearly and explains how they shape outcomes.
