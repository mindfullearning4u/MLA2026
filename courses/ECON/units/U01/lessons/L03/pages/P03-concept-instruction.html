Concept Instruction

Every society must answer three basic economic questions: What will be produced? How will it be produced? and For whom will it be produced? An economic system is the structure a society uses to answer those questions.

In a traditional economy, decisions are based on customs and habits. People often do work that has been done the same way for generations.

In a command economy, the government controls major decisions about production, prices, and distribution.

In a market economy, buyers and sellers make most decisions. Prices, competition, and consumer demand guide production.

In a mixed economy, individuals and businesses make many decisions, but the government also plays an important role through laws, services, and regulation.

🧠Think: If people and governments both influence economic decisions, which system does that most closely describe?

⚠️Common mistake: Assuming real economies fit only one system perfectly. Most modern economies are mixed.
