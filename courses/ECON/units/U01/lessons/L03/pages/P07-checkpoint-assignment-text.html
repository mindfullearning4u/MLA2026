Lesson 3 Checkpoint - Economic Systems Notebook Evidence

This checkpoint verifies your ability to identify an economic system, explain its characteristics, and justify your answer using economic reasoning.

You must choose ONE of the three scenarios from the guided practice:
- A farming village produces goods the same way families have done for generations and passes jobs from parent to child.
- A government decides how much steel, food, and fuel the country will produce and sets the prices for those goods.
- A country allows private businesses to operate freely but also funds schools, roads, and safety inspections.

Required evidence:
- The scenario you selected (must match one of the three options).
- The economic system shown in the scenario.
- An explanation of which details support your answer.
- Who is making most economic decisions in that system.
- One sentence explaining why that system affects production and distribution.

Accepted submission formats:
- Clear photo(s) of handwritten notebook pages
- PDF file
- Typed document

Your Teacher of Record will review this submission for completion and accuracy.

If the scenario is not one of the three provided options, or if the economic system and explanation are unclear, the checkpoint is not complete.

This checkpoint contributes to unit mastery and progression.
