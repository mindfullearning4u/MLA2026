Worked Example

💡 Read the sample scenario and observe how an economic system influences decision-making.

Scenario: A town decides how to provide transportation for its residents.

Traditional economy: People rely on long-standing local methods, such as animal transport or shared community travel patterns.

Command economy: The government decides what transportation services will exist, who receives them, and how much they cost.

Market economy: Private companies offer transportation services based on profit, customer demand, and competition.

Mixed economy: Private companies provide some transportation, but the government also funds roads, public buses, or safety rules.

Why this works:

  • It shows that every system answers the same economic questions differently.
  • It compares the role of tradition, government, and private choice.
  • It explains why most real-world systems combine multiple features.
