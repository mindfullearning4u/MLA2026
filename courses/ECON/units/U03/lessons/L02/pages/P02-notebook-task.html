Unit 03 Lesson 2 - Unemployment and Labor Markets

Write these required notes in your notebook:

  • Unemployment: the condition in which people in the labor force are willing and able to work but do not have jobs.
  • Labor market: the system in which workers look for jobs and employers look for workers.
  • Frictional unemployment: short-term unemployment that happens when people are changing jobs or entering the workforce.
  • Structural unemployment: unemployment caused by changes in skills, technology, or the types of jobs available.
  • Cyclical unemployment: unemployment caused by a slowdown in the economy that reduces overall demand for workers.
  • Workforce trends: changes over time in the kinds of jobs available, worker skills, and labor force participation.
  • Key idea: unemployment can happen for different reasons, and labor markets change as the economy changes.
