Worked Example

Read the sample scenario and observe how business cycle phases affect economic activity.

Scenario: Over several years, stores in a town see rising sales, then weaker sales, and later stronger sales again.

Expansion: At first, stores sell more goods and some businesses open new locations.

Peak: Sales reach their highest level before slowing down.

Recession: Customers spend less, and businesses reduce orders and delay plans.

Trough: Economic activity reaches its lowest level.

Recovery: Spending begins to rise again, and stores start to improve sales.

Why this works:

  • It identifies each major phase of the business cycle.
  • It shows how economic activity changes over time.
  • It connects the cycle to real business behavior in a community.
