Lesson 1 Checkpoint - GDP Notebook Evidence

This checkpoint verifies your ability to define GDP, identify a GDP component, and explain how GDP measures economic output.

You must choose ONE of the three scenarios from the guided practice:
- Families spend more money on groceries and transportation during the year.
- A company buys new computers and expands its office building.
- The government increases spending on public schools and road repairs.

Required evidence:
- The scenario you selected (must match one of the three options).
- A correct definition of GDP.
- The GDP component shown most clearly in the scenario.
- An explanation of why that activity counts as part of GDP.
- One sentence explaining how the activity reflects economic output.

Accepted submission formats:
- Clear photo(s) of handwritten notebook pages
- PDF file
- Typed document

Your Teacher of Record will review this submission for completion and accuracy.

If the scenario is not one of the three provided options, or if GDP and the component explanation are not clearly identified, the checkpoint is not complete.

This checkpoint contributes to unit mastery and progression.
