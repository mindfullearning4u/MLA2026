Worked Example

Read the sample scenario and observe how GDP components are identified.

Scenario: During one year, families buy more clothing and meals, a business opens a new warehouse, the government repairs highways, and the country exports more farm products than it imports.

Consumption: Families buying clothing and meals adds to consumption.

Investment: A business opening a new warehouse adds to investment.

Government spending: Highway repairs add to government spending.

Net exports: Exporting more farm products than importing goods increases net exports.

GDP effect: All of these activities increase total economic output measured by GDP.

Why this works:

  • It identifies all four major GDP components.
  • It connects each component to a realistic economic activity.
  • It shows how GDP reflects total production in the economy.
