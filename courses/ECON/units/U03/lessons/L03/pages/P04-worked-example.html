Worked Example

Read the sample scenario and observe how inflation affects purchasing power.

Scenario: A student usually spends $15 each week on snacks and drinks. Over time, prices rise, and the same items now cost $20.

What changed? The prices of the items increased.

Inflation effect: The student's money does not go as far as it did before.

Purchasing power: With $15, the student can now buy fewer snacks and drinks than before.

Cost of living connection: When many everyday prices rise, it becomes harder for households to afford the same goods and services.

Why this works:

  • It shows a clear increase in prices over time.
  • It explains how rising prices reduce purchasing power.
  • It connects inflation to everyday spending decisions.
