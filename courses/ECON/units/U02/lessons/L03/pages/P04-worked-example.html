Worked Example

💡 Read the sample scenario and observe how equilibrium changes when demand shifts.

Scenario: A local store sells portable fans during a hot summer week. At first, the price and quantity sold are balanced.

Initial market condition: The store is at equilibrium because the quantity demanded equals the quantity supplied.

What changed? A heat wave begins, and many more customers want fans.

Demand shift: Demand increases because consumer need and interest increase.

Result: At the old price, there is a shortage because customers want more fans than the store has available.

New equilibrium: The store may raise the price, and the market moves to a new equilibrium with a higher price and a greater quantity sold if more fans can be supplied.

Why this works:

  • It shows the starting point of equilibrium clearly.
  • It explains how a demand shift creates a shortage at the old price.
  • It connects a market change to a new equilibrium outcome.
