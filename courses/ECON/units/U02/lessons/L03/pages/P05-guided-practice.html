Guided Practice

Practice identifying equilibrium, surplus, shortage, and market changes. Read the situations below and complete the required notebook work.

📘 Notebook Accountability:
Your Teacher of Record will check your notebook for completion of this guided practice. Incomplete or missing work will require revision before you may proceed.

Choose ONE scenario:

  • The price of school hoodies is set too high, and many hoodies remain unsold after several weeks.
  • A sudden increase in demand for bottled water leaves store shelves nearly empty at the current price.
  • A new farming technology helps farmers produce more strawberries, increasing supply in the market.

📘 Notebook Directions (Required):

  1. Write the scenario you selected.
  2. State whether the scenario shows a surplus, a shortage, or a shift toward a new equilibrium.
  3. Explain what happened to supply or demand.
  4. Explain what is likely to happen to price if the market adjusts.
  5. Write one sentence explaining why the market outcome makes economic sense.

⚠️ Reminder: A surplus happens when sellers offer more than buyers want at the current price. A shortage happens when buyers want more than sellers offer at the current price.
