Worked Example

Read the sample scenario and observe how multiple Unit 06 concepts are used together.

Scenario: Country A produces coffee efficiently and exports it. Country B produces machinery efficiently and exports it. The countries trade with each other, and businesses in both countries depend on the exchange.

Trade basics: Coffee is an export for Country A and an import for Country B. Machinery is an export for Country B and an import for Country A.

Comparative advantage: Each country appears to specialize in the product it can produce more efficiently relative to other options.

Interdependence: Both countries rely on the trade relationship to receive important goods.

Economic systems: The way each country organizes production may influence how businesses and trade decisions operate.

Conclusion: The trade relationship works because specialization, exchange, and economic connection support both countries.

Why this works:

  • It connects imports, exports, and specialization in one scenario.
  • It explains how comparative advantage supports trade.
  • It shows how interdependence and system differences can shape international interaction.
