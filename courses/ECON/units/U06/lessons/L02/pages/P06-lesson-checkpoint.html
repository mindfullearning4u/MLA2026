Lesson Checkpoint | Comparative Advantage Notebook Evidence
This checkpoint verifies your ability to identify comparative advantage and explain how trade can improve efficiency.

Important: You must choose ONE of the three scenarios from the guided practice:

  • Country A gives up fewer oranges when producing wheat, while Country B gives up fewer wheat units when producing oranges.
  • Country A gives up less steel when producing cars, while Country B gives up fewer cars when producing steel.
  • Country A gives up less cotton when producing coffee, while Country B gives up less coffee when producing cotton.

Checkpoint Submission Requirements (Required):

  • The scenario you selected (must match one of the three options).
  • Which country has comparative advantage in the first good.
  • Which country has comparative advantage in the second good.
  • An explanation of how specialization could improve efficiency.
  • One sentence explaining why trade could benefit both countries.

If the scenario is not one of the three provided options, or if comparative advantage and efficiency are not clearly explained, the checkpoint is not complete.

Submit your notebook evidence in the assignment titled "Lesson 2 Checkpoint - Comparative Advantage Notebook Evidence" that follows this lesson.
