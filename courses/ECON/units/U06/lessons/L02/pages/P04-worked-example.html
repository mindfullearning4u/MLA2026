Worked Example

Read the sample scenario and observe how comparative advantage supports trade.

Scenario: Country A and Country B both produce wheat and cloth. Country A gives up less cloth when producing wheat. Country B gives up less wheat when producing cloth.

Comparative advantage: Country A has comparative advantage in wheat. Country B has comparative advantage in cloth.

Specialization: Each country should focus more on the good it produces at lower opportunity cost.

Trade benefit: After specializing, the countries can trade and both receive more of the goods they want.

Efficiency: Resources are used more effectively when production is based on comparative advantage.

Why this works:

  • It focuses on opportunity cost rather than total production alone.
  • It shows why specialization can improve efficiency.
  • It explains how both sides can benefit from trade.
