Guided Practice

Practice identifying comparative advantage and explaining why trade can increase efficiency.

Notebook Accountability:
Your Teacher of Record will check your notebook for completion of this guided practice. Incomplete or missing work will require revision before you may proceed.

Choose ONE scenario:

  • Country A gives up fewer oranges when producing wheat, while Country B gives up fewer wheat units when producing oranges.
  • Country A gives up less steel when producing cars, while Country B gives up fewer cars when producing steel.
  • Country A gives up less cotton when producing coffee, while Country B gives up less coffee when producing cotton.

Notebook Directions (Required):

  1. Write the scenario you selected.
  2. Identify which country has comparative advantage in the first good.
  3. Identify which country has comparative advantage in the second good.
  4. Explain how specialization could improve efficiency.
  5. Write one sentence explaining why trade could benefit both countries.

Reminder: Comparative advantage is based on lower opportunity cost, not just producing more units.
