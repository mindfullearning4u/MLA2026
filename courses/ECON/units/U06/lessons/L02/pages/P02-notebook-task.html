Unit 06 Lesson 2 - Comparative Advantage

Write these required notes in your notebook:

  • Comparative advantage: the ability to produce a good or service at a lower opportunity cost than another producer.
  • Opportunity cost: the value of the next best alternative given up.
  • Specialization: focusing on the good or service a producer can make more efficiently compared with other options.
  • Trade benefit: trade can help each side gain by specializing and exchanging.
  • Efficiency: using resources in a way that increases productive output.
  • Key idea: comparative advantage helps explain why trade can benefit both countries even when one country can produce more of everything.
