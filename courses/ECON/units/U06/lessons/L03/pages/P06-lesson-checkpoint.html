Lesson Checkpoint | Global Markets and Interdependence Notebook Evidence
This checkpoint verifies your ability to explain how countries are connected through global markets and interdependence.

Important: You must choose ONE of the three scenarios from the guided practice:

  • A clothing brand designs products in one country, manufactures them in another, and sells them worldwide.
  • A food company depends on cocoa beans grown abroad and ships chocolate products to many countries.
  • A car company uses metal, computer chips, and tires from different countries to build vehicles.

Checkpoint Submission Requirements (Required):

  • The scenario you selected (must match one of the three options).
  • One example of a global market in the scenario.
  • An explanation of how the scenario shows interdependence.
  • A description of one way globalization makes the activity possible.
  • One sentence explaining how a disruption in one country could affect the others.

If the scenario is not one of the three provided options, or if global markets, globalization, and interdependence are not clearly explained, the checkpoint is not complete.

Submit your notebook evidence in the assignment titled "Lesson 3 Checkpoint - Global Markets and Interdependence Notebook Evidence" that follows this lesson.
