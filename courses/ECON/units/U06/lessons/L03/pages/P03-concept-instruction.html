Concept Instruction

A global market is a market that connects buyers and sellers across countries. Goods, services, labor, and resources can move through these global connections.

Globalization is the process through which countries become more connected economically. Improvements in transportation, communication, and technology have increased the speed and scale of global interaction.

Interdependence means countries depend on one another. One country may rely on another for raw materials, manufactured goods, technology, or transportation services.

Because countries are connected, economic events in one nation can affect people and businesses in other nations. A factory shutdown, a shipping delay, or a change in trade patterns can create effects across many markets.

Global interdependence can provide benefits such as wider product choice, access to resources, and larger markets for producers. It can also increase vulnerability when disruptions spread across countries.

In a global economy, countries are linked through trade and production networks. This means economic decisions are often connected rather than isolated.

🧠Think: If a country depends on imports for computer parts, what might happen if those imports are delayed?

⚠️Common mistake: Thinking countries act independently all the time. In global markets, many countries depend on each other economically.
