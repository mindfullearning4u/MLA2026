Lesson Checkpoint | International Trade Basics Notebook Evidence
This checkpoint verifies your ability to identify imports, exports, and specialization in a trade example.

Important: You must choose ONE of the three scenarios from the guided practice:

  • A country sells corn to other countries and buys smartphones from abroad.
  • A country produces large amounts of oil and trades for machinery made elsewhere.
  • A country manufactures shoes and buys coffee beans from another country.

Checkpoint Submission Requirements (Required):

  • The scenario you selected (must match one of the three options).
  • One import from the scenario.
  • One export from the scenario.
  • An explanation of what the country appears to specialize in.
  • One sentence explaining why trade is useful in that scenario.

If the scenario is not one of the three provided options, or if the import, export, and specialization explanation are unclear, the checkpoint is not complete.

Submit your notebook evidence in the assignment titled "Lesson 1 Checkpoint - International Trade Basics Notebook Evidence" that follows this lesson.
