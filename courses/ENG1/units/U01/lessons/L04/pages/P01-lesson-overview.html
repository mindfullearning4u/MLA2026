Lesson 4: Vocabulary in Context

This lesson teaches one concept: context clues allow you to infer meaning without a dictionary. Readers use the words and ideas around an unfamiliar word to make a defensible meaning choice.

This matters because strong readers do not stop every time they meet an unfamiliar word. They gather evidence from nearby sentences and use that evidence to infer meaning while maintaining comprehension.

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Define context clues as nearby words, phrases, and ideas that help reveal meaning.
  • Infer the meaning of an unfamiliar word using textual evidence.
  • Explain which clue in the sentence or paragraph supports the inference.
  • Avoid guessing a meaning with no evidence from the passage.
