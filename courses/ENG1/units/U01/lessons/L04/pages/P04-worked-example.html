💡Worked Example

💡Read the sentence and follow the step-by-step reasoning used to infer a word meaning from context.

Sentence: The trail was steep and rocky, so hikers moved with cautious steps as they climbed.

Step 1: Read the sentence around the word.
Reasoning: I need to use the nearby information, not guess from the word alone.

Step 2: Notice the clue.
Clue: steep and rocky
Reasoning: These words suggest danger or difficulty, so the word cautious must describe careful movement.

Step 3: Infer the meaning.
Meaning: careful to avoid danger or mistakes
Reasoning: That meaning fits both the word and the situation in the sentence.

✅Why this works:

  • The inference is based on a visible clue from the text.
  • The explanation shows how the clue narrows the meaning.
  • The final meaning fits the sentence logically.
