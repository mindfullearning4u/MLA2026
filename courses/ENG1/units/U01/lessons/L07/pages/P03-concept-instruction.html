Unit 01 | Lesson 7

Concept Instruction

Definition: Monitoring comprehension means checking your understanding while you read and noticing when the meaning becomes unclear.

How concepts work together: This lesson builds from earlier reading work. Active reading helps you leave visible thinking in the text. Central idea helps you test whether the paragraph still makes sense overall. Vocabulary in context helps when one unfamiliar word disrupts meaning. These skills work together when confusion appears.

How to apply it: First, identify the exact problem. Are you confused by a word, a sentence, or the overall point? Then choose one fix-up strategy that matches that problem. Reread if the sentence is dense, use context clues if a word is unclear, or restate the central idea if the paragraph starts to feel unfocused.

🧠Think: Why is it stronger to choose one specific fix-up strategy than to keep reading and hope the confusion disappears?

⚠️Common mistake: Saying “I got confused” without identifying what caused the confusion or which strategy would help fix it.
