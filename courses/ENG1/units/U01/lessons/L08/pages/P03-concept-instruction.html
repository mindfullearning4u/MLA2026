Unit 01 | Lesson 8

Concept Instruction

Integrated routine: A strong reader uses Unit 01 skills as a connected process rather than as separate tasks.

How the routine works: The reader begins by reading actively and marking the text. Then the reader decides what the text is mostly saying, which details matter most, and whether any vocabulary or structure affects understanding. If confusion appears, the reader chooses a fix-up strategy. At the end, the reader can explain the text objectively.

Decision-making matters: Not every passage requires the same reading moves in the same amount. Skilled readers decide which tools are most important for the specific text in front of them and adjust when comprehension weakens.

🧠Think: If a passage includes an unfamiliar word, unclear structure, and a difficult main point, how might a reader decide what to address first?

⚠️Common mistake: Treating the routine like a checklist to finish instead of a set of decisions that support understanding.
