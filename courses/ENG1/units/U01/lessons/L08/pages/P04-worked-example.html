💡Worked Example

💡Read the scenario and notice how the reader chooses and combines Unit 01 skills to understand the text.

Scenario: A student is assigned a short article about why cities add more bike lanes. The article explains that bike lanes can reduce traffic, improve safety, and encourage more people to travel without cars. One paragraph uses the word congested, and another compares streets before and after the bike lanes were added.

Reader's integrated routine:
I annotate the reasons the article gives for adding bike lanes. I identify the central idea as: The article argues that bike lanes help cities by improving traffic flow, safety, and transportation choices. I select the details about reduced traffic and safer streets because they directly support that idea. When I reach congested, I use the nearby traffic description to infer that it means crowded or blocked with too many cars. I also notice a compare-and-contrast structure in the paragraph that describes streets before and after the change. When one sentence feels unclear, I reread it and check whether it fits the central idea. At the end, I can summarize the article objectively because I have tracked the main point, details, vocabulary, and structure together.

✅Why this works:

  • The reader chooses several Unit 01 skills based on the needs of the passage.
  • The explanation shows decision-making rather than a fixed list of steps.
  • The final understanding depends on combining multiple skills into one routine.
