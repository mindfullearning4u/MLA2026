📓 Write this heading in your notebook:
Lesson 2 — Central Idea

📓 Write these required notes in your notebook:

  • Definition: The central idea is what the text is mostly saying. It is supported by multiple details across the text.
  • Topic vs. Central Idea: A topic is one or two words. A central idea is a complete sentence that explains the author’s message.
  • Non-negotiable rule: If you write only a topic, you have not identified the central idea.

📓 Write this sentence frame in your notebook (required):

  • Central Idea Frame: The author shows that ____________________ by ____________________ and ____________________.

These notes matter because every later reading skill depends on identifying the correct central idea. If the central idea is wrong, supporting details, summaries, and explanations will also be wrong.
