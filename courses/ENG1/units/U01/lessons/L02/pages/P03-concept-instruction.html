Clarification of Topic and Central Idea

Students often confuse topic and central idea. This mistake leads to weak comprehension and inaccurate analysis.

  • Topic: a category or subject (example: teamwork)
  • Central idea: a full sentence explaining what the author says about the topic

A central idea must apply to the entire text. If it can only be supported by one moment or one paragraph, it is not a valid central idea.

📓 Write this in your notebook:

  • Central idea test: Can I support this idea using details from more than one part of the text?
  • Avoid this mistake: A topic is not a central idea.
