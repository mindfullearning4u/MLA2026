💡Worked Example

💡Read the short passage and follow the reasoning used to identify the central idea.

Passage: School libraries do more than store books. They give students quiet places to read, offer access to research tools, and help classes find materials for projects. When libraries are well used, they become an important part of how students learn independently and with support.

Step 1: Name the topic.
Topic: school libraries
Reasoning: The passage is clearly about libraries, but the topic alone is not the central idea.

Step 2: Look across the whole passage.
The passage mentions quiet reading, research tools, project materials, and student learning.
Reasoning: These details all point to the same larger message.

Step 3: Write the central idea as a full statement.
Central idea: School libraries support student learning by giving access to resources, space, and academic help.
Reasoning: This statement fits the entire passage, not just one sentence.

✅Why this works:

  • The answer is a complete statement, not a one-word topic.
  • The statement connects multiple details from across the passage.
  • The central idea explains what the text is mostly saying overall.
