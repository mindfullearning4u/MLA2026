Lesson 1 Checkpoint – Active Reading Notebook Evidence
This assignment verifies your understanding of active reading and your ability to produce clear notebook evidence.

You must submit evidence of your notebook work from Lesson 1: Active Reading in MLA.

What to Submit (Required):
Submit clear evidence of your notebook work that includes ALL of the following:
• CI: One sentence identifying the central idea of the practice text
• SD: Two supporting details copied directly from the text
• VOC: One important or unfamiliar word with its meaning inferred from context
• ?: Two purposeful margin questions that show active thinking
• Reflection Sentence: One sentence explaining why active reading matters in MLA

Accepted formats:
• Clear photo(s) of handwritten notebook pages
• PDF file
• Typed document

Grading (10 Points Total):
This assignment is graded on completion and accuracy, not length.
• 10 points: All required elements are present and clearly labeled
• 7–9 points: One element is weak or unclear
• 0–6 points: Multiple required elements missing or incomplete

Revisions are required if mastery evidence is incomplete.

Purpose:
This checkpoint confirms that you can actively engage with a text and produce evidence of thinking.
Your Teacher of Record uses this evidence to determine readiness for the next lesson.
