Lesson 1 — Active Reading in MLA

📓 Write these required notes in your notebook:

  • Definition: Active reading means I interact with the text by annotating, asking questions, and tracking my understanding as I read.
  • Purpose: Active reading helps me extract meaning, notice important ideas, and prove my understanding with evidence.
  • Non-negotiable rule: In MLA, reading without annotation and questions is not considered complete work.

📓 Write this annotation key in your notebook:

  • CI = central idea (what the text is mostly saying)
  • SD = supporting detail (evidence that supports the central idea)
  • VOC = unfamiliar word or important word in context
  • ? = a question you have while reading
  • ! = something important or surprising

These notes matter because every future reading task, checkpoint, and unit assessment assumes you can annotate and track your thinking. If you skip this foundation, the rest of the unit will collapse.
