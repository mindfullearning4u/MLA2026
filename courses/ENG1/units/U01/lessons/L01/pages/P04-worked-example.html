Worked Example

👀 Read the text below. You will use it to practice active reading.

Text:
Many students believe that reading is simply moving their eyes across words. However, strong readers treat reading as a conversation. They pause to notice what matters, ask questions when ideas are unclear, and mark key information so they can return to it later. When readers do not interact with the text, they often forget what they read and struggle to explain it. Active reading creates a record of thinking that improves comprehension and supports long-term learning.

Model of active reading (what a strong reader writes in the margins):

  • CI: Reading should be active and interactive, not passive.
  • SD: “strong readers treat reading as a conversation”
  • SD: “Active reading creates a record of thinking”
  • VOC: comprehension (meaning: understanding)
  • ? What does “conversation” mean in this context?
  • ! Active reading supports long-term learning.

Notice what this model proves: the reader identified meaning, selected key details, marked vocabulary, and asked a question that deepens understanding. That is active reading evidence.
