UNIT 01 | LESSON 1

Guided Practice

Practice the active reading process. Complete each item in your notebook by showing one annotation, one question, and one short thinking note.

📒Notebook Accountability:
Your Teacher of Record will check your notebook for completion of this guided practice. Incomplete or missing work will require revision before you may proceed.

Item 1

Passage: Readers understand more when they stop to notice what matters instead of hurrying through every line.

Do this: Underline the most important words, then write one question about the sentence.

Item 2

Passage: Margin notes help readers return to their earlier thinking and check whether their understanding changes.

Do this: Write one annotation and one sentence that tracks how the line helps you understand active reading.

Item 3

Passage: A reader who never asks questions may miss confusion until the entire passage is finished.

Do this: Write one margin question that would help clarify the sentence, then explain why asking it is part of active reading.

📝Notebook Directions (Required):

  1. Label your work Item 1, Item 2, and Item 3.
  2. For each item, include the exact annotation, question, or thinking note you would write.
  3. Use short, specific responses that prove you are interacting with the text while reading.

⚠️Reminder: Active reading shows visible thinking. Do not just copy the sentence. Mark it, question it, and explain your thinking.
