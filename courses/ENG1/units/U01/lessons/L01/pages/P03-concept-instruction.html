Concept – Active Reading in MLA

Active reading is not the same as highlighting random lines. Active reading requires three actions:

  • Annotate: mark what matters using a consistent key.
  • Question: ask purposeful questions that help you understand meaning and structure.
  • Track understanding: notice when you understand and when you do not, then respond.

Passive reading sounds like this: “I read it.” Active reading produces evidence: marked text and written thinking. Your TOR cannot verify learning without evidence.

📓 Write this in your notebook:

  • Active reading produces evidence: annotations + questions + clear tracking of understanding.
  • Passive reading produces no evidence: no notes = no proof of learning.
  • Purposeful margin questions: questions that help me identify meaning, importance, or confusion.

Examples of purposeful margin questions (write these in your notebook):

  • What is the author trying to show here?
  • Why does this detail matter?
  • How does this connect to the central idea?
  • What does this word mean in this sentence?
  • What is unclear, and what should I reread?
