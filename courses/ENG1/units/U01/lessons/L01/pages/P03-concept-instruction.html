Concept Instruction

Definition: Active reading is a deliberate reading process. Instead of just moving through words, the reader stops to mark important ideas, ask questions, and record thinking.

Explanation: A strong reader interacts with the text while reading. Annotation helps the reader notice what matters. Questions help the reader identify confusion or importance. Tracking thinking helps the reader remember how meaning develops across the passage.

How to apply it: As you read, underline or label important lines, write short notes in the margin, and pause to ask what the author is saying, what seems important, and what needs clarification. If your thinking changes, write that change down.

🧠Think: If a reader finishes a paragraph but cannot explain what stood out, what does that suggest about the reader's process?

⚠️Common mistake: Highlighting large parts of the text without writing any thinking. Active reading requires visible reasoning, not just marking words.
