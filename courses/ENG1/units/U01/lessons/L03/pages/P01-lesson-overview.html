Lesson 3: Supporting Details

This lesson teaches one concept: strong supporting details are relevant, specific, and directly connected to the central idea. Good details do not just appear in the text; they do clear evidence work.

This matters because readers must judge whether a detail truly supports the main point. Weak details distract, repeat, or do not clearly connect to what the text is mostly saying.

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Define strong supporting details as relevant, specific, and connected to the central idea.
  • Distinguish between a useful supporting detail and an unrelated detail.
  • Explain how a detail supports a central idea.
  • Select text evidence that directly connects to the main point.
