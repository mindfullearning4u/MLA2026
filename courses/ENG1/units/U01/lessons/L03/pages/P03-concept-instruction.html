Concept Instruction

Definition: Supporting details are the specific facts, examples, or explanations that develop the central idea.

Explanation: A strong detail must be relevant to the central idea, specific enough to add real evidence, and directly connected to the text's main point. A random fact from the passage is not automatically a strong supporting detail.

How to apply it: First identify the central idea. Then ask whether the detail clearly helps prove, explain, or develop that idea. If the detail could disappear without changing the main point, it is probably weak support.

🧠Think: Why is the sentence with the most interesting information not always the best supporting detail?

⚠️Common mistake: Picking a detail because it sounds important, even though it does not directly support the central idea.
