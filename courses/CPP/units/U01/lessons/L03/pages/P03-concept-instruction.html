Concept | Career Clusters and Pathways

There are many career options, and it can be difficult to compare them one at a time. Career clusters help students organize those options into broader categories based on similar skills, interests, or work settings.

A career cluster is a large category of related occupations. Examples include health science, information technology, education and training, business management, manufacturing, and arts and communication.

A career pathway is a more specific group of jobs within a cluster. For example, the health science cluster may include pathways such as patient care, medical support, or health administration.

Learning about clusters and pathways helps students explore options more efficiently. Instead of focusing on one job title, students can examine a full category of related careers and notice patterns in education, job tasks, and work environments.

Career clusters do not force a student into one choice. They provide a structure for exploration and help students connect personal interests and strengths to broader career areas.

🧠 Think: Which career cluster seems most connected to your interests, strengths, or work preferences so far?

⚠️ Common mistake: Treating a single job title as the only option. One career cluster may include many different roles and pathways.
