Concept Instruction

A strong career plan starts with self-awareness. Students need to understand what they enjoy, what they do well, and which tasks feel meaningful or motivating.

Interests are the activities, subjects, or experiences that capture attention and curiosity. For example, a student who enjoys building, fixing, or designing may be interested in engineering, construction, or technology careers.

Strengths are abilities that come more naturally or have been developed through practice. A student might have strengths in communication, organization, problem-solving, teamwork, creativity, or leadership.

A self-assessment helps students recognize patterns in their interests and strengths. These patterns can guide future career exploration by showing which career clusters or job tasks might be a good fit.

Self-assessment does not choose a career for a student. Instead, it gives useful information that supports better decisions about future goals and options.

🧠Think: Which school subjects, hobbies, or activities make you feel interested, confident, or successful?

⚠️Common mistake: Assuming one strength or one interest decides your entire future. Self-assessment is a starting point for exploration, not a final answer.
