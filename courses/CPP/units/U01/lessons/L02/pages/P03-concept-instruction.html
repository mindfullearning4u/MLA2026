Concept | Values and Work Preferences

Career decisions are stronger when students think about what matters most to them. A job may match a person's interests, but it may still feel like a poor fit if it does not match that person's values or work preferences.

Values are the priorities or beliefs that guide decisions. For example, a student may value helping others, job stability, creativity, independence, teamwork, or work-life balance.

Work preferences describe the type of environment or conditions a person wants. A student may prefer working indoors or outdoors, working with people or independently, having a predictable schedule, or having opportunities to lead.

Understanding values and preferences helps students compare career options more carefully. It also helps explain why one career may feel like a better fit than another, even when both seem interesting.

Career planning is not only about what a student can do. It is also about the type of work and lifestyle a student wants to build.

🧠 Think: What matters most to you in a future job: helping others, stability, creativity, independence, teamwork, or something else?

⚠️ Common mistake: Choosing a career based only on popularity or salary without considering whether the work matches your values and preferences.
