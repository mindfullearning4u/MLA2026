Concept Instruction

Career research is stronger when students use data instead of assumptions. Job outlook and salary analysis help students understand whether a career is growing, how much workers may earn, and whether the field seems stable or competitive.

Main ideas in this lesson:

  • Demand: shows how much employers need workers in a field.
  • Growth trends: show whether a field is expected to expand, stay stable, or shrink over time.
  • Earning potential: gives students a general idea of what workers may earn in a career.

Outlook and salary data should not be the only reasons a student chooses a career. They are important pieces of information that should be compared with interests, values, and pathway requirements.

Students make stronger decisions when they combine personal fit with career data.
