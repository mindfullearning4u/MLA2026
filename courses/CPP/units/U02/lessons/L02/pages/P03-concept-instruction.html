Concept Instruction

Education and training pathways are the routes people take to prepare for careers. Not every career requires the same kind of preparation. Some careers require a college degree. Others require technical training, certification, or a combination of different experiences.

Main pathway types:

  • Degrees: often completed through a college or university program.
  • Certifications: show that a person has completed training or demonstrated a specific skill.
  • Technical training: focused, career-related learning that prepares students for specific jobs.

Students should ask clear questions during career research. What kind of training is needed? How long does it take? Is there a required credential? Does the pathway match the student's goals and plans?

Understanding pathways helps students make realistic decisions. It also helps students compare careers by more than interest alone.
