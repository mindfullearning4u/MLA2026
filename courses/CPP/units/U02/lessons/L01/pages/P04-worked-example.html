💡 Worked Example

Scenario: Jordan is interested in healthcare but is not sure which role to explore first.

Step 1: Jordan chooses one career to research first: physical therapist assistant.

Step 2: Jordan looks for three kinds of information:

  • What the worker does each day
  • What education or training is required
  • What skills seem important in the role

Step 3: Jordan records the information clearly:

  • Job role: helps physical therapists support patient care
  • Responsibilities: working with patients, following treatment plans, and tracking progress
  • Requirements: specialized training and certification

Step 4: Jordan uses the research to decide whether this career should stay on the list of possible options.

This example shows that career research helps students move from a general interest area to more specific, useful information.
