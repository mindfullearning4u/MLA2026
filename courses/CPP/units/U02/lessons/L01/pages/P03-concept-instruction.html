Concept Instruction

Career research helps students move from general interest to specific information. Instead of guessing what a career is like, students can learn what workers actually do, what skills are important, and what preparation is expected.

Important ideas in career research:

  • Job role: the position or type of work a person does.
  • Responsibilities: the regular tasks connected to that role.
  • Requirements: the qualifications needed to enter or succeed in the role.
  • Research source: a place students can gather reliable information, such as a career website, school counselor, or training program page.

Good career research answers clear questions. What does this worker do? What skills are important? What training is needed? What does a typical day look like? The more specific the questions are, the more useful the information will be.

Career research does not force a final decision. It helps students compare options using facts. This makes future planning more realistic and more informed.
