Unit 02 | Lesson 4

Concept Instruction

After students gather information about careers, they still need to evaluate their choices. Evaluation means comparing options carefully instead of making a quick decision.

Students can compare careers by looking at:

  • Job roles and responsibilities
  • Education and training requirements
  • Outlook and salary information
  • Personal interests, values, and work preferences

An informed decision does not mean a student is finished forever. It means the student can explain a current choice clearly using evidence.

Strong career decisions are based on both personal fit and research information.
