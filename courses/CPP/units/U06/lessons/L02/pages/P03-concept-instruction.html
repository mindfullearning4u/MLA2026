Concept Instruction

Revision is the process of improving work after reviewing it. In a career portfolio, revision may involve fixing weak wording, improving organization, adding explanations, or replacing weaker artifacts with better evidence.

Why revision matters:

  • It improves quality
  • It helps work become clearer and more professional
  • It shows responsibility and effort
  • It helps students respond to feedback instead of ignoring it

Useful revision questions:

  • Is this clear?
  • Does this support my purpose?
  • Is this my strongest evidence?
  • Did I explain why this artifact matters?

Feedback is most useful when you apply it. A comment from a teacher, peer, or reviewer can point out what is missing or unclear. Revision turns that feedback into a stronger final product.

In professional settings, revision is normal. People often improve resumes, presentations, and portfolios multiple times before final use.
