🧠 Concept Instruction

Reflection is an important part of career development because it helps you evaluate your progress instead of only finishing tasks. When you reflect, you think carefully about what you have learned, what you can do, and what still needs attention.

What reflection includes:

  • Looking back at work you completed
  • Identifying strengths shown in that work
  • Recognizing mistakes or weak areas
  • Deciding what to improve next

Examples of growth evidence:

  • A stronger resume draft than an earlier version
  • Better organization in a portfolio
  • Improved communication in presentations or writing
  • More confidence when describing goals and skills

Reflection is most useful when it is specific. Instead of saying, "I got better," a stronger reflection explains what improved and what evidence proves it.

Reflection also helps you prepare for future decisions. When you know your strengths and areas for improvement, you can make better choices about revision, presentation, and next steps.
