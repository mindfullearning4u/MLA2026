💡 Worked Example

Scenario: Jalen planned to join a career-related club, but the club schedule now conflicts with an important class support session.

Step 1: Jalen identifies the barrier: a schedule conflict.

Step 2: Jalen thinks about possible adjustments, such as choosing a different activity, meeting with the advisor another time, or revising the timeline.

Step 3: Jalen selects a new plan that still supports the larger career goal.

Step 4: Jalen writes a short explanation of how the adjustment helps maintain progress.

This example shows that strong planning includes the ability to adjust when obstacles appear.
