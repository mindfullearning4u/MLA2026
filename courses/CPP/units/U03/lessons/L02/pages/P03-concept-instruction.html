Concept Instruction

Students often think about the future in a general way, but strong planning requires smaller steps. Short-term planning focuses on what can be done soon. Long-term planning focuses on where a student wants to go over a longer period of time.

Important planning ideas:

  • Short-term steps: actions a student can take now or soon
  • Long-term direction: the larger result a student hopes to reach later
  • Timeline: the order of steps that connects present actions to future outcomes

A timeline can help students stay organized. It shows what should happen first, what should happen next, and what long-term results those steps support.

Good planning helps students connect school actions, career preparation, and future opportunities.
