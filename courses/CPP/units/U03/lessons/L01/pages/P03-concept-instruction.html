Concept Instruction

Goals are more useful when they are clear. A SMART goal gives a student a stronger plan because it explains what needs to happen, how progress will be checked, and when the goal should be completed.

SMART stands for:

  • Specific: clear and focused
  • Measurable: trackable
  • Achievable: realistic
  • Relevant: connected to the student's plans
  • Time-bound: connected to a deadline

A weak goal might say, "I want to do better in school." A stronger SMART goal explains exactly what the student wants to improve, how progress will be measured, and by when.

SMART goals help students plan for both school success and career readiness.
