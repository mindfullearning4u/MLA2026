💡 Worked Example

Scenario: Nina wants to improve her public speaking because she is interested in leadership roles.

Weak goal: "I want to get better at speaking."

Step 1: Make the goal specific.
Nina changes it to: "I will improve my class presentation skills."

Step 2: Make the goal measurable and achievable.
Nina decides she will practice once each week and present more clearly by using note cards and eye contact.

Step 3: Make the goal relevant and time-bound.
Nina connects the goal to leadership and sets a deadline for the end of the quarter.

Final SMART goal: "By the end of the quarter, I will improve my presentation skills by practicing once each week and using clear notes and eye contact during class presentations."

This example shows how a general idea becomes a stronger goal when all SMART parts are included.
