Unit 06 | Lesson 3

Concept Instruction

Citizenship means being a member of a community with both rights and responsibilities. In the modern world, these responsibilities extend beyond local neighborhoods and national borders because people are connected through technology, communication, and global events.

One important part of modern citizenship is digital citizenship. Digital citizenship means using technology responsibly, communicating respectfully online, and thinking carefully about the information we share and consume.

People today can participate in civic life through online discussions, digital campaigns, and access to information. However, these opportunities also create responsibilities. Citizens should verify information, avoid spreading false claims, and respect others in digital spaces.

Citizenship in a changing world also means understanding that local actions can connect to wider issues. Economic challenges, environmental concerns, and public health issues can affect many communities at once. Responsible citizenship includes awareness, participation, and ethical behavior.

Modern citizenship still includes traditional responsibilities such as obeying laws, respecting others, and contributing to the community. What has changed is the scope and speed of interaction in daily life.

🧠Think: How can a person act responsibly online while also contributing positively to society?

⚠️Common mistake: Do not assume citizenship only matters during elections. Responsible citizenship also includes everyday behavior, online conduct, and informed participation.
