Worked Example

Scenario:
A student reads a dramatic post online about a public issue. Before sharing it, the student checks whether the information comes from a reliable source and decides not to repost misleading content.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
Sharing information online is not part of citizenship because it only happens on the internet.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
This situation shows digital citizenship. The student is acting responsibly by checking information before sharing it. Responsible citizenship includes careful decision-making in online spaces because digital actions can affect other people and public understanding.

Why this works:

  • It identifies responsible online behavior.
  • It connects digital choices to citizenship responsibilities.
  • It explains why verifying information matters.
  • It shows how online actions can affect society.
