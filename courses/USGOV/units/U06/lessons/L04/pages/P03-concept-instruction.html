Concept: Evaluating Policy and Government Decisions

Evaluating government action means carefully analyzing what a policy or decision does and whether it achieves its intended purpose. This process helps determine if a policy is working as expected.

One important factor is effectiveness. A policy is effective if it successfully solves or improves the problem it was designed to address.

Another factor is fairness. Fair policies treat individuals and groups appropriately and do not create unnecessary inequality or harm.

It is also important to consider consequences. Policies can have both intended outcomes and unintended effects. Some decisions may solve one problem while creating new challenges.

Citizens should evaluate government actions by examining evidence, considering different perspectives, and understanding both benefits and drawbacks.

Think: Can a policy be effective but still be considered unfair? Why?

Common mistake: Do not judge a policy based only on one outcome. Consider effectiveness, fairness, and consequences together.
