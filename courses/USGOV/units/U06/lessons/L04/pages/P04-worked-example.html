Worked Example

Scenario:
A government creates a policy to reduce traffic congestion by adding tolls to busy roads. Traffic decreases, but some people argue the policy is unfair to lower-income drivers.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
The policy is successful, so no further evaluation is needed.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
The policy is effective because it reduces traffic, but it must also be evaluated for fairness. The financial impact on different groups is an important consideration. The policy may have unintended consequences that should be addressed.

Why this works:

  • Identifies effectiveness clearly
  • Examines fairness across groups
  • Recognizes unintended consequences
  • Applies evaluation criteria to a real situation
