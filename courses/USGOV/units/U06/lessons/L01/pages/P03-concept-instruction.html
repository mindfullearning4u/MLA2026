Concept: Public Policy Process

Public policy is developed through a process that begins when government identifies a problem. Issues such as education, healthcare, transportation, or safety often require government action.

The next step is creating a policy. This usually involves lawmakers proposing and passing laws or government leaders making decisions about how to address the issue.

After a policy is created, it must be implemented. Implementation means putting the policy into action through programs, regulations, or services.

Finally, government evaluates the policy. Officials review whether the policy is effective, fair, and achieving its intended goal. Based on evaluation, policies may be continued, changed, or removed.

Public policy matters because it directly affects people’s lives. Decisions about funding, rules, and services shape communities and influence opportunities.

💭 Think: What problem might require government to create a new policy?

⚠️ Common mistake: Do not assume policy creation ends with passing a law. Implementation and evaluation are also essential steps.
