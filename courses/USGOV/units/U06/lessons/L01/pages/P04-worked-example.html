Worked Example

Scenario:
A city identifies traffic safety as a problem. Lawmakers create a policy requiring lower speed limits and install traffic signals. The city later reviews accident rates to see if the policy worked.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
The policy process ended when the law was created.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
The policy process includes identifying the problem, creating the policy, implementing it through changes like signals, and evaluating results such as accident rates.

Why this works:

  • Identifies each step in the policy process
  • Connects policy to real-world action
  • Shows implementation and evaluation clearly
  • Explains impact on public safety
