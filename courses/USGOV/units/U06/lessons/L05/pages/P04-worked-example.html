Worked Example

Scenario:
A government introduces a policy to improve access to education by increasing funding for schools and providing resources for students. After implementation, test scores improve, but some communities argue that funding is not distributed equally.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
The policy is successful because test scores improved, so no further analysis is needed.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
The policy addresses an important issue and shows effectiveness through improved outcomes. However, it must also be evaluated for fairness, as unequal distribution of resources may create concerns. Both effectiveness and fairness must be considered.

Why this works:

  • Identifies the issue clearly
  • Explains government response
  • Evaluates effectiveness and fairness
  • Recognizes potential consequences
