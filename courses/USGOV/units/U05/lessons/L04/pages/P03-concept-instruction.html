Concept: Civic Participation Beyond Voting

Civic participation includes any action that allows individuals to contribute to their community or influence government. While voting is one of the most direct forms, there are many other ways to participate.

People can participate through community service, such as volunteering to improve local conditions. They can also participate through advocacy, which involves supporting causes, raising awareness, and influencing decisions.

Another form of participation is communicating with government officials. Citizens can share opinions, ask questions, and provide feedback about policies and decisions.

These actions matter because they help shape communities and influence how government responds to issues. Participation beyond voting allows individuals to stay engaged and contribute to change over time.

Think: How can someone influence government decisions without voting?

Common mistake: Do not assume voting is the only way to participate. Civic participation includes many different actions.
