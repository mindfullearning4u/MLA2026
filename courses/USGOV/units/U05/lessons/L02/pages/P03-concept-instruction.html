Concept Instruction

Political parties are organized groups that work to elect candidates and influence government leadership. Parties bring together people with similar ideas about government, laws, and public policy. In elections, political parties support candidates who represent their goals.

Interest groups are groups that try to influence government decisions and public policy on specific issues. Unlike political parties, interest groups do not usually focus on electing candidates to run the government. Instead, they advocate for causes such as education, business interests, environmental issues, or public health.

Political parties and interest groups are similar because both try to shape government. However, they do so in different ways. Parties work broadly through elections and leadership, while interest groups usually focus more narrowly on specific goals and advocacy efforts.

These groups matter because they help people organize their voices. They provide ways for citizens to support policies, promote ideas, and influence government outside of casting a single vote.

🧠Think: Why might a person join an interest group instead of a political party?

⚠️Common mistake: Do not assume political parties and interest groups are the same. Parties focus on electing candidates and leading government, while interest groups focus on influencing policy around specific issues.
