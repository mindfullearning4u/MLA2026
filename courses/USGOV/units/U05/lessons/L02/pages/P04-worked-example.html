💡Worked Example

Scenario:
A group works to support candidates in elections and helps organize voters around broad political goals. Another group focuses only on changing one environmental policy and meets with lawmakers to advocate for that issue.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
Both groups are political parties because they are involved in politics.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
The first group is a political party because it works to elect candidates and organize voters around broad goals. The second group is an interest group because it focuses on one issue and tries to influence policy decisions.

✅Why this works:

  • It distinguishes elections from policy advocacy.
  • It identifies the broader role of parties.
  • It identifies the issue-based role of interest groups.
  • It explains how both groups influence government differently.
