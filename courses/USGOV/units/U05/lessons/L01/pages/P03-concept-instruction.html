Concept: Voting and Elections

Elections are the process by which citizens choose their leaders. In the United States, elections are held regularly to select officials such as the president, members of Congress, and local leaders.

Voting is a form of civic participation. It gives citizens a direct role in shaping government. When people vote, they express their preferences and help determine the direction of policies and leadership.

A democratic system depends on participation. If citizens do not vote, fewer voices are represented in government decisions. High participation leads to a government that better reflects the population.

Elections must be free and fair. This means people can vote without pressure, and votes are counted accurately. Trust in elections is essential for a stable government.

💭 Think: What might happen if many citizens choose not to vote?

⚠️ Common mistake: Do not assume voting is the only form of participation, but recognize it is one of the most important.
