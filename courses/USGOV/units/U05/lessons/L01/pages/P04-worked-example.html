Worked Example

Scenario:
A community is voting for a new mayor. Only a small number of eligible voters participate in the election.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
The election result is still fully representative because a vote happened.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
This situation shows low civic participation. When fewer people vote, the results may not represent the views of the entire community. Participation is necessary for elections to reflect the population accurately.

Why this works:

  • Identifies the role of participation
  • Explains why turnout matters
  • Connects voting to representation
  • Applies the concept to a real situation
