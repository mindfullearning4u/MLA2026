Concept: Applying Civic Participation

This lesson requires you to apply what you have learned about civic participation. You will analyze a situation and determine how different actions affect government and democracy.

When analyzing a scenario, identify the type of participation involved. This may include voting, involvement in political parties, participation in interest groups, or civic actions beyond voting.

Next, explain how the action influences government. Some actions directly affect elections, while others influence policies, opinions, or community conditions.

Finally, evaluate the impact. Strong participation helps create a government that reflects the people and responds to issues.

Think: How do different forms of participation affect democracy in different ways?

Common mistake: Do not only describe the action. You must explain its impact on government or society.
