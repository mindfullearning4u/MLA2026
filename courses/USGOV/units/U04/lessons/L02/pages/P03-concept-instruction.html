Concept: Liberties vs. Rights

Civil liberties are freedoms that protect individuals from government interference. These include rights such as freedom of speech, religion, and privacy.

Civil rights are protections that ensure individuals are treated equally and are not discriminated against based on characteristics such as race, gender, or religion.

For example, freedom of speech is a civil liberty because it protects your ability to express ideas. Equal access to public services is a civil right because it ensures fairness and prevents discrimination.

Both civil liberties and civil rights are essential for a fair and just society. They work together to protect both freedom and equality.

Think: Can a society have freedom without equality? Why or why not?

Common mistake: Do not confuse liberties with rights. Liberties protect freedom from government, while rights ensure equal treatment.
