Concept: Applying Rights and Liberties

In this lesson, you will apply what you have learned about rights and liberties. You will not learn new content. Instead, you will use your understanding to analyze a situation and determine what is lawful and fair.

When analyzing a scenario, you must first identify the type of right involved. Ask yourself whether the situation involves civil liberties (freedom), civil rights (equality), due process (fair procedures), equal protection (fair treatment), or limits on rights.

Next, you must determine whether the right is being protected or limited. If it is limited, you must decide whether the limitation is justified based on safety, order, or the rights of others.

Finally, you must clearly explain your reasoning. A strong response identifies the correct concept, explains the situation, and justifies the outcome using accurate reasoning.

💭 Think: How can you tell when a limit on a right is reasonable?

⚠️ Common mistake: Do not simply name a right. You must explain how the situation connects to that right and justify the outcome.
