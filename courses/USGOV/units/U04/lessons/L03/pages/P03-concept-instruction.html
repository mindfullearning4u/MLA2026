Concept: Due Process and Equal Protection

Due process means the government must act fairly when it uses its power against a person. If the government wants to punish someone, take property, or limit liberty, it must follow legal procedures instead of acting without rules.

Equal protection means the law must be applied fairly. People who are in similar situations should not be treated differently without a lawful reason. Equal protection helps guard against unfair discrimination and unequal treatment.

These protections are important because government power affects real people. Due process focuses on fair procedures. Equal protection focuses on fair treatment. Together, they help protect justice under the law.

For example, if a student is punished without being told the reason or without being allowed to respond, that raises a due process issue. If two people are treated differently by the same rule for no valid reason, that raises an equal protection issue.

💭 Think: Why is it important for government to follow fair procedures instead of making decisions however it wants?

⚠️ Common mistake: Do not treat due process and equal protection as the same idea. Due process is about fair procedures. Equal protection is about fair treatment under the law.
