Concept: Limits on Rights

The Constitution protects many important rights, such as freedom of speech and religion. However, these rights are not absolute. The government can place limits on rights when necessary to protect public safety, maintain order, or protect the rights of others.

For example, freedom of speech does not allow someone to threaten others or cause harm. The government may restrict speech in certain situations to prevent danger or protect people.

Courts play an important role in deciding when limits are allowed. They examine whether a restriction is reasonable and whether it protects important public interests without unfairly taking away rights.

The key idea is balance. Rights must be protected, but they must also be used responsibly so that society can function safely and fairly.

Think: When should the government be allowed to limit a right?

Common mistake: Do not assume that having a right means it can never be limited. Rights can be restricted under specific conditions.
