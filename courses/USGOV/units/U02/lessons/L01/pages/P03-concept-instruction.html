Concept Instruction

The Articles of Confederation were the first system of government used by the United States. They created a confederation, meaning the states kept most power while the national government had limited authority.

This system made it difficult for the country to function effectively. The national government could not tax, could not enforce laws, and could not regulate trade between states. As a result, the government struggled to manage money, resolve conflicts, and maintain order.

These weaknesses became clear through real problems, such as unpaid war debts and events like Shaysâ€™ Rebellion, which showed that the government could not respond effectively to unrest. Leaders recognized that a stronger national government was necessary.

⚠️Common mistake: Thinking the Articles of Confederation were designed to be strong. In reality, they were intentionally weak because leaders feared centralized power after British rule.
