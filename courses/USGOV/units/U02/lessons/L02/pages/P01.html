Lesson 2: Core Constitutional Principles

This lesson teaches one concept: the Constitution is built on four core principles that organize and limit government power. These principles are popular sovereignty, limited government, separation of powers, and federalism.

Each principle helps answer an important question about government. Who gives government its power? How is government power restricted? How is power divided? How is authority shared between the national and state governments? In this lesson, you will learn how these principles work together to create a government that has power, but not unlimited power.
