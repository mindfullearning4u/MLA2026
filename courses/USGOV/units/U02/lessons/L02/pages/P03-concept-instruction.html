Concept Instruction

The Constitution is based on four major ideas that shape how the United States government operates. These ideas are called constitutional principles. They are not random rules. They are the foundation of how power is created, divided, and limited in government.

Popular sovereignty means the people are the source of government authority. In a democracy, government power is legitimate because citizens agree to be governed and participate through voting and civic action. Limited government means government officials and institutions must follow the law. The Constitution limits what government can do so power is not absolute.

Separation of powers divides authority among the legislative, executive, and judicial branches. This prevents one branch from controlling all government action. Federalism divides power between the national government and the state governments. This allows both levels of government to have authority in different areas.

🧠Think: Which constitutional principle best explains why no single part of government should control all power?

⚠️Common mistake: Do not confuse separation of powers with federalism. Separation of powers divides authority among the three branches of the national government. Federalism divides authority between the national government and the state governments.
