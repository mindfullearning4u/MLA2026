Worked Example

Scenario:
A mayor says, “I was elected by the people, so I should be able to make any decision I want without limits from laws or courts.”

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
Because the mayor was chosen by voters, the mayor has unlimited power.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:

This statement misunderstands constitutional principles. Popular sovereignty does mean that power comes from the people. However, limited government means elected leaders still must obey the law. Government officials cannot do anything they want simply because they were elected.

Why this works:

  • It correctly identifies popular sovereignty as power coming from the people.
  • It correctly applies limited government by showing that leaders must follow laws and rules.
  • It shows that constitutional principles work together, not separately.
