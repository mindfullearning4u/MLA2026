Heading:

Core Constitutional Principles

  • Popular sovereignty means government gets its power from the people.
  • Limited government means government must obey the law and does not have unlimited power.
  • Separation of powers means government power is divided among different branches.
  • Federalism means power is shared between the national government and state governments.
  • These principles prevent too much power from being held in one place.
  • These principles help protect liberty, order, and balanced government.
