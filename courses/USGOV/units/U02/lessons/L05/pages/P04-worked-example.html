Worked Example

Scenario:
A state government passes a law that directly conflicts with a law passed by the national government.

Question:
How should this situation be analyzed using constitutional principles?

Modeled Response:
This situation involves federalism and the relationship between state and national power. When state and national laws conflict, national law generally has priority. This means the state law may be invalid because it conflicts with national authority.

Why this works:

  • It identifies the correct concept: federalism
  • It applies the principle of national authority over conflicting state laws
  • It explains the reasoning clearly
  • It connects the scenario to constitutional structure
