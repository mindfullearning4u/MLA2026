Worked Example

Scenario:
A state government decides the rules for public schools in that state. At the same time, the national government is responsible for defending the country through the military.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
Both of these responsibilities belong to the national government because it is the highest level of government.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
This scenario shows federalism. Public education is generally a reserved power, so it belongs to state government authority. National defense is a delegated power, so it belongs to the national government.

Why this works:

  • It correctly identifies that the Constitution divides power between levels of government.
  • It correctly classifies public education as a state responsibility.
  • It correctly classifies national defense as a national responsibility.
  • It shows that federalism organizes government by assigning authority to different levels.
