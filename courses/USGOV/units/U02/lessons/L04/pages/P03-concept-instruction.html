Concept Instruction

An amendment is a formal change to the Constitution. The Framers understood that the country would face new conditions over time, so they created a process that allows the Constitution to be updated without replacing the entire document.

The amendment process has two main stages. Proposal means that an amendment is officially suggested. Ratification means that the amendment is officially approved. These steps require strong support because the Constitution is the highest law in the country.

The process is intentionally difficult. If the Constitution could be changed too easily, the government could become unstable and basic rights could be weakened by quick political changes. The amendment process creates a balance: it allows change when needed, but it requires broad agreement before that change becomes part of the Constitution.

🧠Think: Why is it important for the Constitution to allow change, but not allow change too easily?

⚠️Common mistake: Do not confuse an amendment with a regular law. A regular law can be passed through the normal lawmaking process, but an amendment changes the Constitution itself and requires a much harder approval process.
