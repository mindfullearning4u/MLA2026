Worked Example

Scenario:
A group of citizens believes that a new protection should apply to every person in every state, and they want that protection to become part of the Constitution.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
Congress can just pass a regular law, and that will permanently change the Constitution.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
A regular law does not change the Constitution. If the goal is to add a permanent constitutional protection for all people across the country, the change would require an amendment. That means the change must first be proposed and then ratified.

Why this works:

  • It correctly identifies that an amendment changes the Constitution, not a regular law.
  • It correctly applies the two major stages: proposal and ratification.
  • It shows that constitutional change requires broad support.
  • It explains why a national constitutional change must follow the amendment process.
