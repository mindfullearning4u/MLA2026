Applying What You Learned

Scenario

A new student organization at your school wants to create rules for meetings and decision-making. Some members believe there should be no rules so everyone is free to do what they want. Others believe the group needs clear rules, leadership roles, and a fair way to make decisions.

Directions

Read the scenario carefully. Then answer each question using complete sentences. Use what you learned in this lesson to explain your thinking.

  1. What is the main problem this student organization is facing?
  2. How does the purpose of government connect to this situation?
  3. Why are rules and structure important for this group?
  4. How could authority and power be used fairly to help the group make decisions?

Submission Requirements

  • Write your answers in complete sentences.
  • Use lesson vocabulary when appropriate.
  • Clearly explain your reasoning.
  • Make sure your response shows your understanding of the lesson concept.
Checkpoint Reminder: This checkpoint is used to show your understanding before moving forward. Take your time, review the lesson if needed, and make sure your answers are clear and complete.
