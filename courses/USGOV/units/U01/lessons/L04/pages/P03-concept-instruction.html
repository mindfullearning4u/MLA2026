Concept: Citizenship and Civic Responsibility

Citizenship means being a recognized member of a country. Citizens have both rights and responsibilities.

Rights are freedoms and protections, such as freedom of speech or the right to vote. These rights allow individuals to participate in society.

Responsibilities are duties that citizens are expected to fulfill. These include following laws, respecting others, and contributing to the community.

Civic responsibility means actively participating in society in ways that support the common good. This can include voting, staying informed, and helping the community.

Governments function effectively when citizens understand and carry out both their rights and responsibilities.

💭 Think: Why is it important for citizens to both have rights and follow responsibilities?

⚠️ Common mistake: Thinking citizenship only includes rights. Responsibilities are equally important for a functioning society.
