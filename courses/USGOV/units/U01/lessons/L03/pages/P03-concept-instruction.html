Concept Instruction

Power is the ability to influence or control people, actions, or decisions. Governments use power to enforce laws and maintain order.

Authority is different from power. Authority means that people recognize the governmentâ€™s right to use power. When authority is accepted, people are more likely to follow rules willingly.

Legitimacy is when people believe that a governmentâ€™s power and authority are rightful and justified. Legitimacy is important because it creates trust and stability.

If a government has power but lacks legitimacy, people may resist or refuse to follow laws. If a government has legitimacy, citizens are more likely to cooperate.

🧠🧠Think: Why are people more willing to follow rules they believe are fair?

⚠️⚠️Common mistake: Thinking power and authority are the same. Power is control, but authority is the accepted right to use that control.
