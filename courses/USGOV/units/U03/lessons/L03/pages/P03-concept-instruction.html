Concept Instruction

The judicial branch is one of the three branches of the national government. Its main job is to interpret laws and apply them to specific cases. This means courts decide what laws mean and how they should be used in real situations.

The judicial branch includes many courts, but the most important is the Supreme Court. The Supreme Court makes final decisions on important legal and constitutional questions.

One of the most important powers of the judicial branch is judicial review. Judicial review allows courts to decide whether a law follows the Constitution. If a law does not follow the Constitution, the court can declare it unconstitutional.

The judicial branch is different from the other branches. Congress makes laws. The president enforces laws. Courts interpret laws. Each branch has a separate role in the constitutional system.

🧠Think: Why is it important for courts to have the power to review laws for constitutionality?

⚠️Common mistake: Do not confuse interpreting laws with making laws. Courts explain and apply laws, but they do not create them.
