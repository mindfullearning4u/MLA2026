Worked Example

Scenario:
A law is challenged in court because some citizens believe it violates the Constitution. The case reaches the Supreme Court, where justices review the law and decide whether it is constitutional.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
This is mainly the job of Congress because Congress created the law, so it should decide if the law is correct.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
This scenario shows the judicial branch in action. Courts, especially the Supreme Court, interpret laws and determine whether they follow the Constitution. This process is called judicial review.

Why this works:

  • It identifies the courts as responsible for interpreting laws.
  • It correctly explains judicial review.
  • It distinguishes the role of the judicial branch from the legislative branch.
  • It shows how the Constitution is protected through court decisions.
