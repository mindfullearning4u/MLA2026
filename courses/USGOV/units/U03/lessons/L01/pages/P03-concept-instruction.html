Concept: The Legislative Branch

The legislative branch is one of the three branches of the national government. Its main job is to make laws. At the national level, the legislative branch is called Congress.

Congress is made up of two chambers. The House of Representatives is based on population, so states with more people have more representatives. The Senate gives equal representation to each state, with two senators per state. This structure balances the interests of both large and small states.

Congress does more than vote on laws. It also debates ideas, represents different groups, and helps shape national policy. Because members of Congress are elected, the legislative branch connects citizens to the lawmaking process through representation.

💭 Think: Why might the Constitution include both the House of Representatives and the Senate instead of only one lawmaking body?

⚠️ Common mistake: Do not assume that the legislative branch only “talks about laws.” Its central constitutional role is to make laws, and both chambers of Congress must participate in that process.
