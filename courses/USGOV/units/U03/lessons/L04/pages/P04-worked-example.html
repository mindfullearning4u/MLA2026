💡Worked Example

Scenario:
Congress passes a law. The president reviews the law and rejects it. Later, Congress reviews the decision and attempts to pass the law again.

Not Responsible / Incorrect Thinking:
Only Congress is responsible because it created the law, so it has full control over what happens next.

Correct / Responsible Breakdown:
This scenario shows checks and balances in action. Congress creates laws, but the president can limit that power by rejecting the law. Congress can then respond, showing that both branches influence each other.

✅Why this works:

  • It shows interaction between branches.
  • It demonstrates that no single branch has complete authority.
  • It explains how power is limited through shared actions.
  • It connects directly to the idea of checks and balances.
