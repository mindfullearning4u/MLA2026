Concept Instruction

The system of checks and balances is designed to keep power balanced among the three branches of government. Each branch has its own role, but it also has powers that allow it to limit the actions of the other branches.

For example, Congress (legislative branch) can pass laws, but the president (executive branch) can approve or reject those laws. Courts (judicial branch) can review laws and decide if they follow the Constitution. These interactions ensure that no branch has complete control.

Checks and balances work together with separation of powers. While separation of powers divides responsibilities, checks and balances creates interaction and limits between the branches.

This system is important because it prevents abuse of power. By requiring branches to share influence, the Constitution protects democratic government and the rights of citizens.

🧠Think: What could happen if one branch had no limits on its power?

⚠️Common mistake: Do not confuse checks and balances with separation of powers. Separation divides roles. Checks and balances allows branches to limit each other.
