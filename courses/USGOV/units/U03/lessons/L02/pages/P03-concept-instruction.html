Concept: The Executive Branch

The executive branch is one of the three branches of the national government. Its main job is to carry out and enforce the laws that have been created. At the national level, the executive branch is led by the president.

The president has several leadership roles. The president helps guide national action, responds to important issues, and oversees the executive branch as it carries out the law. This branch includes the people and agencies responsible for putting laws into action.

The executive branch is different from the legislative branch and the judicial branch. Congress makes laws. Courts interpret laws. The executive branch enforces laws. Understanding this difference helps explain how the branches have separate constitutional roles.

💭 Think: Why is it important for one branch to focus on enforcing laws instead of making them?

⚠️ Common mistake: Do not confuse the executive branch with the legislative branch. The president may influence government decisions, but the executive branch does not create laws in the same way Congress does.
