Concept Instruction

In this lesson, you will apply what you have learned about the three branches of government. Instead of learning new information, you will use your understanding to analyze situations and identify which branch is responsible.

The legislative branch creates laws through Congress. The executive branch carries out and enforces those laws through the president. The judicial branch interprets laws and determines whether they follow the Constitution.

In real situations, it is important to carefully identify the action being taken. If a law is being created, the legislative branch is responsible. If a law is being enforced, the executive branch is responsible. If a law is being interpreted or reviewed, the judicial branch is responsible.

You must use precise reasoning when identifying branches. Simply naming a branch is not enough. You must explain how the action in the scenario matches the role of that branch.
