Concept | Short-Term and Long-Term Financial Goals

Financial goals give direction to financial decisions. When a person knows what they are working toward, it becomes easier to compare options and avoid decisions that slow progress.

Short-term goals may include saving for a school expense, a needed device repair, or a small emergency fund. Long-term goals may include a car purchase, college costs, or building larger savings over time.

Strong financial goals usually answer these questions:

  • What am I saving for?
  • How much money is needed?
  • When do I want to reach the goal?
  • What decision choices support the goal?

A vague goal such as "save more money" is weaker than a measurable goal such as "save $200 in four months by setting aside $50 each month."

⚠️ Common mistake: setting a goal without checking whether the amount and timeline are realistic.
