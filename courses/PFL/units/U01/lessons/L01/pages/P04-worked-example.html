💡 Worked Example | Comparing Two Choices

Scenario: Ava has $60.

  • Option A: spend $60 on concert merchandise today
  • Option B: save the $60 toward a $150 laptop repair she will need soon

Step 1: Identify the trade-off.
If Ava buys the merchandise, she cannot keep the $60 for the repair fund.

Step 2: Identify the opportunity cost.
If Ava chooses the merchandise, the opportunity cost is the progress she could have made toward the repair.

Step 3: Compare consequences.

  • Short-term benefit of A: immediate enjoyment
  • Long-term problem of A: still needs the full $150 later
  • Short-term result of B: no purchase today
  • Long-term benefit of B: already has $60 of the $150 needed

Best-supported decision: Option B is the stronger financial choice because it supports a known future expense and reduces financial stress later.
