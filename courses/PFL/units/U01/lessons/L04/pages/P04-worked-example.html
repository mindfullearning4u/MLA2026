💡 Worked Example | Using a Decision Strategy

Scenario: Nora needs headphones for online coursework.

  • Option A: $18 headphones with weak reviews
  • Option B: $32 headphones with stronger reviews and longer expected use

Step 1: Compare cost.
Option A costs $14 less.

Step 2: Compare value.
Option B may last longer and support school use more reliably.

Step 3: Compare consequences.
If Option A breaks quickly, Nora may need to spend money again. If Option B lasts longer, the higher price may still be the stronger value.

Best-supported decision: Option B is the stronger financial decision if the higher quality reduces replacement cost and better supports a real need.
