💡Worked Example | Ranking Priorities

Scenario: Leo has $90 this week.

  • Bus pass: $40
  • Club event ticket: $25
  • Streaming subscription renewal: $15
  • Snacks with friends: $10

Step 1: Identify the need.
The bus pass supports transportation to school and work, so it is the strongest priority.

Step 2: Identify wants.
The club ticket, subscription, and snacks are wants because they are optional.

Step 3: Compare consequences.
If Leo skips the bus pass, transportation becomes a problem. If Leo delays the subscription, the impact is smaller.

Best-supported decision: Leo should pay the $40 bus pass first, then decide whether the remaining $50 should be split between one want and savings instead of buying every want.
