Concept | Needs, Wants, and Financial Priorities

Financial decision-making is stronger when a person can tell the difference between what is necessary and what is optional. This helps people decide how to use limited money responsibly.

A need is usually connected to safety, health, transportation, or another basic responsibility. A want may still have value, but it is not the first financial priority when money is limited.

Priorities depend on the situation. A person may want a new item for entertainment, but a transportation cost, phone bill, or school fee may need to come first.

Strong financial priorities are based on:

  • what must be paid
  • what supports goals
  • what happens if a payment is delayed
  • what offers the strongest value over time

⚠️ Common mistake: calling every desired purchase a need in order to justify spending.
