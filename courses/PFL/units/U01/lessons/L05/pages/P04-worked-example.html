💡 Worked Example | Full Scenario Analysis

Scenario: Tia has $120.

  • She needs $50 for a school trip fee due next week.
  • She wants to spend $45 on new shoes that are not required.
  • She also wants to begin saving for a $200 laptop upgrade.

Analysis:

  • The school trip fee is the strongest need and priority.
  • The shoes are a want, not the first priority.
  • If Tia buys the shoes, she keeps less money for both the fee and the laptop goal.
  • The opportunity cost of buying the shoes is slower progress on the goal and less flexibility for the required fee.

Best-supported decision: Tia should pay the $50 school fee first and place part of the remaining money into savings for the laptop upgrade before choosing any optional purchase.
