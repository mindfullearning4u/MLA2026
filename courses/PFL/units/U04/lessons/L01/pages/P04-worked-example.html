💡 Worked Example | Comparing Two Credit Behaviors

Scenario: Student A pays borrowed amounts on time each month. Student B misses several payments and ignores due dates.

Step 1: Compare repayment behavior.
Student A shows consistent responsibility. Student B shows inconsistent repayment behavior.

Step 2: Compare likely long-term effect.
Student A is more likely to build stronger borrowing trust over time. Student B is more likely to weaken future borrowing opportunities.

Best-supported conclusion: On-time repayment is a stronger credit behavior because it supports future financial options and reduces borrowing risk.
