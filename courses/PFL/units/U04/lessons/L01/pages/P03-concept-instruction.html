Concept Instruction

Credit allows a person to borrow money now and repay it later. Because credit involves risk, lenders often use a credit score to help judge whether the borrower is likely to repay responsibly.

Credit behavior can be shaped by:

  • paying on time
  • borrowing responsibly
  • avoiding patterns of missed payments
  • making decisions based on long-term impact

A stronger credit record can support better borrowing opportunities later. A weaker credit record can limit options or increase costs.

This means credit decisions are not just about getting money now. They are also about how present choices affect future financial outcomes.

⚠️Common mistake: focusing only on immediate access to money without thinking about repayment responsibility and future borrowing impact.
