Concept | Interest, Fees, and Cost of Borrowing

Borrowing money creates cost. The total cost is not only the original borrowed amount. Interest and fees can increase how much the borrower must repay.

Strong borrowing analysis asks:

  • How much is being borrowed?
  • How much interest will be added?
  • Are there any extra fees?
  • What is the full repayment amount?

A borrowing option with a lower starting amount is not automatically better if its interest or fees are much higher. The strongest comparison uses the full repayment total.

This lesson focuses on numerical reasoning because credit decisions should be supported by total cost evidence.

⚠️ Common mistake: comparing loans by the borrowed amount alone and ignoring the added cost over time.
