Concept | Types of Credit and Loans

Different borrowing tools exist because financial needs are not all the same. A strong credit decision compares the purpose of the borrowing to the type of credit being used.

Examples include:

  • credit cards for purchase access
  • personal loans for a set borrowing amount
  • student loans for education expenses
  • mortgages for property purchases

The strongest borrowing option depends on the financial purpose, repayment expectations, and long-term responsibility connected to that type of loan.

Comparing options matters because choosing the wrong type of credit can create unnecessary risk or cost.

⚠️ Common mistake: choosing a borrowing option only because it is available instead of because it fits the financial purpose.
