💡 Worked Example | Analyzing a Borrowing Scenario

Scenario: A borrower needs $600 for a necessary expense. Option A adds $54 in interest and a $12 fee. Option B adds $38 in interest and no fee.

Step 1: Calculate total repayment for Option A.
$600 + $54 + $12 = $666

Step 2: Calculate total repayment for Option B.
$600 + $38 + $0 = $638

Step 3: Compare the totals.
Option B costs $28 less overall than Option A.

Step 4: Evaluate the borrowing decision.
If both options meet the same need, the lower total cost is the stronger choice, especially when the borrower must manage repayment carefully.

Best-supported conclusion: Option B is the stronger borrowing option because it lowers total repayment and reduces long-term financial pressure.
