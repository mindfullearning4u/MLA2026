Concept Instruction

Investment decisions involve both possibility and uncertainty. Return describes possible growth, while risk describes the possibility that the investment may not perform as hoped.

Strong risk analysis asks:

  • What is the possible reward?
  • What is the possible downside?
  • How much depends on one single choice?
  • Would spreading the investment lower the risk?

Diversification means spreading money across more than one investment option instead of relying on only one. This can reduce the impact of one weak result.

⚠️Common mistake: focusing only on possible return and ignoring the level of risk involved in the investment decision.
