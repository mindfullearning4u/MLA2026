💡 Worked Example | Comparing Diversified and Non-Diversified Choices

Scenario: Investor A places all money into one investment. Investor B spreads the same amount across several investments.

Step 1: Compare concentration of risk.
Investor A depends on one result. Investor B spreads the risk across multiple choices.

Step 2: Compare likely stability.
Investor B is less dependent on one single investment outcome than Investor A.

Best-supported conclusion: Investor B is using the stronger diversification strategy because the risk is spread across multiple investments.
