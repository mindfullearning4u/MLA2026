Concept | Compound Interest and Long-Term Growth

Compound growth happens when money grows and then future growth builds on that earlier increase. Over time, this can make long-term growth stronger than simple growth alone.

Strong long-term growth analysis considers:

  • the starting amount
  • the amount of growth over time
  • the length of the investment period
  • how growth can build on earlier gains

Time matters because the longer the money can grow, the more opportunities there are for additional gains to build on earlier gains.

⚠️ Common mistake: underestimating how much earlier investing can change long-term financial outcomes.
