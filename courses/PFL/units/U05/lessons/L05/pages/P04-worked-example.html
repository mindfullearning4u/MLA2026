💡 Worked Example | Analyzing an Investment Scenario

Scenario: Investor A chooses one single investment that may grow quickly but depends on one result. Investor B spreads money across multiple investments and expects steadier long-term growth.

Step 1: Compare concentration of risk.
Investor A depends on one option. Investor B spreads risk across more than one option.

Step 2: Compare long-term strength.
Investor B's choice may better support long-term stability because it is less dependent on one outcome.

Step 3: Evaluate the recommendation.
If the goal is long-term growth with lower dependence on one single result, the diversified choice is stronger.

Best-supported conclusion: Investor B is using the stronger overall strategy because the decision considers risk, diversification, and long-term growth together.
