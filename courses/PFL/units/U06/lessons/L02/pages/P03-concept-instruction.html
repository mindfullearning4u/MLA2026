Concept Instruction

Insurance is part of long-term financial planning because it helps reduce the financial impact of unexpected events. Instead of paying the full cost of a loss alone, a person may pay regular premiums to share risk with an insurer.

Basic insurance reasoning considers:

  • what type of loss could happen
  • how costly that loss could be
  • what kind of protection is needed
  • whether the cost of protection is justified by the risk

Insurance does not remove all risk, but it can reduce financial damage when major expenses occur. That is why insurance is connected to responsible financial planning.

⚠️Common mistake: assuming insurance is unnecessary just because a loss has not happened yet.
