Worked Example | Building a Simple Financial Plan

Scenario: A student expects a future training cost, wants transportation access, and is deciding whether to save $120 each month or spend the money on nonessential purchases.

Step 1: Identify the main goal.
The goal is to prepare for a future education or career expense.

Step 2: Compare the two choices.
Saving supports the future goal. Spending now reduces money available later.

Step 3: Calculate the planned savings over 5 months.
$120 x 5 = $600

Best-supported conclusion: The saving choice is stronger because it supports the future plan, reduces financial risk, and reflects responsible decision-making.
