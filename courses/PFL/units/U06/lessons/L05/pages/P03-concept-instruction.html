Assessment Readiness | Financial Plan and Reflection

The Unit 6 assessment asks you to apply financial knowledge rather than repeat definitions. A strong response should compare options, identify likely consequences, and explain which choices best support long-term stability.

Strong unit assessment reasoning should include:

  • a clear financial goal or need
  • comparison of at least two possible choices
  • attention to risk, cost, and responsibility
  • a justified conclusion based on evidence

Reflection matters because it shows whether a financial plan is realistic, responsible, and connected to future priorities.

Common mistake: giving advice without explaining the financial evidence or consequences behind the recommendation.
