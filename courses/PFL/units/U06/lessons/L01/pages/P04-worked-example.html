Worked Example | Comparing Life-Stage Priorities

Scenario: One student plans to save $40 each month for training costs after graduation. Another spends the same $40 each month on nonessential purchases.

Step 1: Identify the future goal.
The first student has a future education or career expense in mind.

Step 2: Compare the choices.
One choice supports a future need, while the other choice uses the money now with no long-term plan.

Step 3: Calculate the planned savings.
$40 x 6 months = $240

Best-supported conclusion: The saving choice better supports life-stage planning because it prepares for a future expense and reduces later financial pressure.
