Unit 06 | Lesson 3

Concept Instruction

Major financial decisions should be made carefully because they can shape financial stability for months or years. Large or repeated expenses can affect saving, debt, and the ability to respond to emergencies.

Strong decision-making in this area considers:

  • the total cost of the choice
  • whether the choice is a need or a lifestyle preference
  • how the choice affects other priorities
  • what long-term consequences may result

Financially responsible choices are not always the cheapest, but they should be supported by clear reasoning, realistic affordability, and awareness of consequences.

⚠️Common mistake: judging a major decision only by the immediate monthly cost without considering the larger financial impact.
