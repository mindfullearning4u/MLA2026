Worked Example | Comparing Transportation Choices

Scenario: One student plans for public transportation at $90 per month. Another chooses a car payment of $260 per month plus $80 for insurance.

Step 1: Calculate the monthly car-related cost.
$260 + $80 = $340 per month

Step 2: Compare the monthly choices.
Public transportation costs $90. The car-related choice costs $340.

Step 3: Find the monthly difference.
$340 - $90 = $250 more each month

Best-supported conclusion: The lower-cost option may better support a limited budget unless there is a clear reason the more expensive option is necessary.
