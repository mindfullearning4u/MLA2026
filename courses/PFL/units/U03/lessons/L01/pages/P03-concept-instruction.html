Concept Instruction

A budget is a financial plan that organizes money coming in and money going out. Budgets support decision-making because they show whether a spending choice fits the available income.

Strong budget thinking includes:

  • listing all income sources
  • listing expected expenses
  • comparing totals
  • adjusting choices when spending is too high

A balanced budget happens when income covers expenses. If expenses are higher than income, a person must change spending, increase income, or lower goals.

Budgeting is not just recording numbers. It is deciding which expenses matter most and using evidence to justify those choices.

⚠️Common mistake: focusing on one expense at a time instead of checking the total budget result.
