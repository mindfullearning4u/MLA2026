Concept Instruction

Taxes are required payments that help fund public services and responsibilities. A person does not keep all earned income because some money may be collected through taxes or other deductions.

Common tax types in basic financial literacy include:

  • income tax on earnings
  • sales tax on some purchases
  • property tax connected to owned property

Taxes matter because they affect real financial outcomes. A paycheck with taxes deducted leads to less take-home income than gross income. A purchase with sales tax costs more than the listed pre-tax price.

Financial responsibility means understanding that taxes are part of real costs and should be considered when comparing options and planning spending.

⚠️Common mistake: planning with pre-tax prices or gross income instead of the actual after-tax result.
