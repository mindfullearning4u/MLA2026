💡 Worked Example | Sales Tax and Final Cost

Scenario: A student wants to buy a calculator priced at $48. Sales tax is $3.84.

Step 1: Identify the listed price.
The calculator costs $48 before tax.

Step 2: Add the tax.
$48 + $3.84 = $51.84 total cost

Step 3: Interpret the decision.
The student must compare the full after-tax cost, not just the listed price, when deciding whether the calculator fits the budget.
