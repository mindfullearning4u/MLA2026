Concept Instruction

Income is the money a person receives from work or other approved sources. Understanding how income is earned helps a person make stronger financial decisions about spending, saving, and planning.

Common types of earned income include:

  • hourly wages for time worked
  • salary as a fixed amount over a period
  • tips added by customers
  • commission based on sales or performance

Hourly income can change if the number of hours worked changes. Salary income is usually more predictable, but both can still be compared using total earnings.

Strong financial reasoning asks:

  • How much is earned?
  • Is the income predictable?
  • How do hours, rate, or fixed pay affect total earnings?
  • What does this mean for future budgeting?

⚠️Common mistake: assuming one job pays more just because the hourly rate looks larger without checking total hours or total earnings.
