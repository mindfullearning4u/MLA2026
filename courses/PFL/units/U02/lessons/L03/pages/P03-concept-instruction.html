Concept | Understanding Paychecks and Deductions

A paycheck shows more than just how much was earned. It also shows how deductions reduce the amount of money a worker receives.

The two most important paycheck terms in this lesson are:

  • gross income = total earnings before deductions
  • net income = final amount received after deductions

Common deductions can include taxes and other required withholdings. Because of deductions, a worker cannot plan a budget using gross income alone.

Strong financial reasoning asks:

  • How much was earned in total?
  • How much was deducted?
  • How much remains as take-home pay?
  • What does that mean for spending and saving decisions?

⚠️ Common mistake: assuming the full gross income is available to spend.
