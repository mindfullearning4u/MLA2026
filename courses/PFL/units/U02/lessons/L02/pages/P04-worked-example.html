💡Worked Example | Comparing Career Income Factors

Scenario: Lana is comparing two options after graduation.

  • Option A: full-time job earning $16 per hour immediately
  • Option B: technical training program for one year, then a job earning $22 per hour

Step 1: Compare starting earnings.
Option A pays sooner because work begins immediately.

Step 2: Compare future earnings.
Option B may lead to higher hourly income after the training year.

Step 3: Evaluate the decision.
If Lana needs immediate income, Option A may be stronger now. If Lana can complete the training and wants higher later earnings, Option B may be stronger long-term.

Best-supported conclusion: The stronger choice depends on whether Lana's priority is immediate income or higher future earnings.
