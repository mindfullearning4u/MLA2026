Concept – Media Analysis & Critical Evaluation

Media analysis requires more than identifying whether information is true or false. It involves examining how a message is constructed, why it was created, and how it may influence an audience.

Bias, emotional language, selective facts, and lack of credible sources can all signal that a message should be questioned. Media-literate individuals evaluate these elements before forming conclusions or sharing content.

Careful analysis helps prevent the spread of misinformation and supports responsible participation in digital spaces.

🧠 Think: Why is it important to verify information before reacting or sharing it online?

⚠️ Common mistake: Focusing only on whether a post feels believable instead of examining evidence and sources.
