Lesson 1 Checkpoint – What Is Media Literacy?
This checkpoint verifies your ability to analyze media messages and explain why critical evaluation is important.

You must choose ONE of the three situations from the guided practice:
• A viral video makes a strong claim but provides no evidence.
• An online article uses emotional language to persuade readers.
• A sponsored post appears to be a news story.

Required evidence:
• The situation you selected (must match one of the three options).
• One reason the message should be questioned.
• An explanation of why the information may be misleading.
• One action a media-literate person would take before accepting the message.
• One sentence explaining why media literacy matters.

Accepted submission formats:
• Clear photo(s) of handwritten notebook pages
• PDF file
• Typed document

Your Teacher of Record will review this submission for completion and accuracy.

If the situation is not one of the three provided options, or if the analysis is unclear, the checkpoint is not met and must be revised.

This checkpoint contributes to unit mastery and progression.
