Concept - What Is Media Literacy?

Media includes news articles, social media posts, videos, advertisements, podcasts, and images. Every media message is created by someone, for a reason, and for a specific audience.

Media literacy is the ability to access, analyze, evaluate, and interpret these messages. Instead of accepting information automatically, media-literate individuals ask questions about accuracy, purpose, and perspective.

Because information spreads quickly online, media literacy is essential for identifying misleading content, emotional manipulation, and incomplete or biased messages.

🧠 Think: How might the same event be presented differently by different media sources?

⚠️ Common mistake: Assuming that popular or frequently shared information is always accurate.
