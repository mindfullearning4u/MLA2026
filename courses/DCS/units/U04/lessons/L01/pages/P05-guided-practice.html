Guided Practice

Practice applying media literacy skills by analyzing how information is presented.

📓 Notebook Accountability:
Your Teacher of Record will check your notebook for completion of this guided practice. Incomplete or missing work will require revision before you may proceed.

Choose ONE situation:

  • A viral video makes a strong claim but provides no evidence.
  • An online article uses emotional language to persuade readers.
  • A sponsored post appears to be a news story.

📓 Notebook Directions (Required):

  1. Write the situation you selected.
  2. Identify one reason the media message should be questioned.
  3. Explain why the information may be misleading.
  4. Describe one step a media-literate person would take before believing it.
  5. Write one sentence explaining why media literacy matters.

⚠️ Reminder: Media literacy requires active thinking, not passive consumption.
