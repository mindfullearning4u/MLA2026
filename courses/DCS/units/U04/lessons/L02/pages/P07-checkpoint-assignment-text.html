Lesson 2 Checkpoint - News Sources, Bias & Loaded Language
This checkpoint verifies your ability to identify bias and explain how loaded language and source choices influence meaning.

You must choose ONE of the three situations from the guided practice:
• An article describes a policy as "dangerous" without evidence.
• A headline exaggerates outcomes to attract clicks.
• A report quotes only one perspective.

Required evidence:
• The situation you selected (must match one of the three options).
• One example of bias or loaded language in the situation.
• An explanation of how the language or source choice influences the reader's interpretation.
• A revised, more neutral version of the message (rewrite the key sentence or headline).
• One sentence explaining why recognizing bias and loaded language matters.

Accepted submission formats:
• Clear photo(s) of handwritten notebook pages
• PDF file
• Typed document

Your Teacher of Record will review this submission for completion and accuracy.

If the situation is not one of the three provided options, or if the bias/loaded language and neutral rewrite are unclear, the checkpoint is not met and must be revised.

This checkpoint contributes to unit mastery and progression.
