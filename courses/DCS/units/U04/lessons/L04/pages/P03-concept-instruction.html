Concept – Fact-Checking & Verification Tools

Fact-checking is the process of confirming whether information is accurate before accepting or sharing it. This includes examining sources, checking evidence, and comparing information across multiple outlets.

Credible sources typically provide clear authorship, reliable evidence, and accountability. When information cannot be traced to a reliable source, it should be questioned.

Verification tools include checking official websites, comparing coverage from trusted news organizations, and looking for original sources of data or claims.

🧠 Think: Why is checking more than one source important?

⚠️ Common mistake: Trusting a claim because it appears professional or well-designed.
