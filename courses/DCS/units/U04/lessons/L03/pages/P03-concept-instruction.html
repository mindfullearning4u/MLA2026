Concept – Misinformation, Disinformation & Algorithms

False information spreads online for different reasons. Sometimes people share incorrect information without realizing it is false. Other times, information is intentionally created to deceive, manipulate opinions, or cause harm.

Misinformation refers to false information shared unintentionally. Disinformation refers to false information created and shared deliberately. Both can spread quickly online.

Algorithms play a major role in this process. They are designed to promote content that receives high engagement. Content that sparks strong emotions—such as fear, anger, or excitement—is more likely to be shown repeatedly, even if it is inaccurate.

🧠 Think: Why might emotionally charged posts spread faster than neutral information?

⚠️ Common mistake: Believing information is accurate simply because it appears repeatedly in a feed.
