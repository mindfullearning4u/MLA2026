Concept – Respectful Online Communication & Digital Etiquette

Online communication relies heavily on tone, even though tone can be difficult to read in text-based messages. Without facial expressions, voice, or body language, messages can easily be misunderstood.

Misinterpretation happens when the reader understands a message differently than the writer intended. Short messages, sarcasm, jokes, or abrupt wording often cause confusion or conflict.

Digital etiquette means communicating respectfully, choosing words carefully, and considering how your message may be received. Different platforms—email, chat, discussion boards, social media—have different expectations for tone, formality, and clarity.

🧠 Think: How might a message that sounds “fine” to you come across as rude, dismissive, or confusing to someone else?

⚠️ Common mistake: Assuming the reader “knows what you mean” without providing enough clarity or context.
