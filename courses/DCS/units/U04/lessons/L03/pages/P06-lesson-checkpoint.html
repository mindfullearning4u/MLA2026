🎯 Lesson Checkpoint – Misinformation, Disinformation & Algorithms
This checkpoint verifies your ability to identify false information and explain how it spreads.

Important: You must choose ONE of the three guided practice situations:

  • A viral post shares an alarming claim but later turns out to be incorrect.
  • A misleading video is created to intentionally influence opinions.
  • A user repeatedly sees the same false claim recommended by their feed.

Checkpoint Submission Requirements (Required):

  • The situation you selected.
  • Whether it represents misinformation or disinformation.
  • An explanation of how algorithms contribute to its spread.
  • One responsible action a digital citizen should take.
  • One sentence explaining why this issue matters.

If the explanation is unclear or does not correctly identify misinformation, disinformation, or algorithm influence, the checkpoint is not met and must be revised.
