Worked Example

👀 Read the scenario and observe how misinformation spreads.

Scenario: A social media post claims a common household item can cure serious illnesses. The post includes dramatic language and personal testimonials but no scientific evidence.

Why the post spreads:

  • The claim triggers hope and fear.
  • Users share it to help others without verifying it.
  • High engagement causes the algorithm to promote it to more users.

Media-literate response:

  • Pause before reacting or sharing.
  • Recognize emotional manipulation.
  • Check reliable medical or scientific sources.

Why this matters:

  • False information can cause real harm.
  • Algorithms do not evaluate truth-only engagement.
  • Critical thinking reduces misinformation spread.
