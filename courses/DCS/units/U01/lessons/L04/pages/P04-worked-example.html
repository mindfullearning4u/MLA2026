Worked Example

Scenario:

Taylor posts a comment on a class discussion board. Another student, Chris, replies: "That makes no sense."

Taylor feels embarrassed and responds quickly with: "At least I tried, unlike you."

Analysis:

  • Chris’s message is dismissive and lacks respectful communication.
  • Taylor reacts emotionally, which escalates the conflict.
  • Both responses contribute to a negative and unproductive interaction.

Better Response Strategy:

Instead of reacting immediately, Taylor could pause and respond with: "Can you explain what part you think needs improvement?"

  • This response seeks clarification rather than escalating conflict.
  • It keeps the conversation respectful and focused on improvement.
  • It demonstrates responsible decision-making.

Conclusion:

This example shows how pausing, choosing respectful language, and asking for clarification can prevent conflict from escalating online.
