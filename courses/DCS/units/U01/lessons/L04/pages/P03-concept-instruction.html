Unit 01 | Lesson 4

Concept Instruction

Online conflict can start quickly because tone is hard to read, emotions escalate fast, and messages spread instantly. A conflict trigger might be a rude comment, a misunderstanding, or a message written in frustration.

Responsible digital citizens use de-escalation strategies such as pausing before responding, asking clarifying questions, or choosing calmer wording. Sometimes the safest choice is to disengage-stop replying, mute the conversation, or step away.

When behavior becomes harmful, threatening, or violates platform rules, students should use reporting tools to protect themselves and others. Ethical decision-making means choosing actions that reduce harm and support a safe digital environment.

🧠Think: What is the safest choice when someone is trying to provoke you online?

⚠️Common mistake: Responding immediately when upset, which often escalates the conflict.
