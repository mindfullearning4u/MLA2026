Concept Instruction

Digital etiquette refers to the standards of respectful and appropriate behavior when communicating online. Because digital communication often lacks facial expressions, tone of voice, and body language, messages can easily be misunderstood.

Tone is the attitude or feeling behind a message. In face-to-face communication, tone is supported by voice and expression. Online, tone must be conveyed through word choice, punctuation, and clarity.

Common challenges in digital communication include:

  • Misinterpretation: A short message may seem rude even if it was not intended that way.
  • Lack of context: Without background information, readers may assume the wrong meaning.
  • Overuse of punctuation: Excessive capitalization or punctuation (like ALL CAPS or !!!) can appear aggressive.
  • Delayed responses: Not responding quickly can sometimes be misunderstood as ignoring someone.

To communicate respectfully online, digital citizens should:

  • Use clear and complete sentences
  • Choose words carefully to avoid misunderstanding
  • Reread messages before sending
  • Consider how the message might be received by others

Practicing strong digital etiquette helps create a positive, respectful, and safe online environment for everyone.
