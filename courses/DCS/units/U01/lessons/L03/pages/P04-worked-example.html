Worked Example

Scenario:

Alex sends a message to a classmate that says: "You didn’t do it right."

The classmate feels upset because the message sounds harsh and critical. However, Alex intended to help and did not mean to sound rude.

Analysis:

  • The message lacks a polite tone and may be interpreted as disrespectful.
  • There is no explanation or supportive language to clarify intent.
  • This is an example of how tone can be misunderstood in digital communication.

Improved Message:

"I think there might be a small mistake. Do you want me to help you fix it?"

  • The message is respectful and supportive.
  • It avoids blame and encourages collaboration.
  • The tone is clear and positive.

Conclusion:

This example shows how small changes in wording can significantly improve communication and prevent misunderstandings.
