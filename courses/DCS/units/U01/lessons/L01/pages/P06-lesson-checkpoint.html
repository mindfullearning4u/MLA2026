🎯 Lesson Checkpoint – Digital Citizenship Notebook Evidence
This checkpoint verifies your ability to identify responsible and irresponsible digital citizenship and explain why digital behavior matters.

Important: You must choose ONE of the three situations from the guided practice:

  • A friend shares a screenshot of a private conversation without permission.
  • A classmate posts a comment that sounds rude, but you are not sure if they meant it that way.
  • You see someone spreading a rumor about another student online.

Checkpoint Submission Requirements (Required):

  • The situation you selected (must match one of the three options).
  • One example of irresponsible digital citizenship in that situation.
  • A revised version of the situation that shows a responsible digital citizenship choice.
  • One sentence explaining why responsible digital behavior matters.

If the situation is not one of the three provided options, or if the responsible and irresponsible choices are not clear, the checkpoint is not met and must be revised.

📓 Submit your notebook evidence in the assignment titled “Lesson 1 Checkpoint – Digital Citizenship Notebook Evidence” that follows this lesson.
