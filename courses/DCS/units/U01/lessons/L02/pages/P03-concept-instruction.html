Concept – Digital Access

Digital access means having the tools needed to participate in digital environments, such as computers, tablets, internet connections, and software. Not everyone has the same level of access.

Some students may have fast internet and personal devices, while others may share devices or have limited internet access. This difference can affect how people learn, communicate, and complete tasks.

Digital citizenship includes recognizing these differences and acting with fairness, patience, and understanding.

🧠 Think: How would learning change if you did not have reliable internet or your own device?

⚠️ Common mistake: Assuming everyone has the same access to technology and can complete tasks in the same way.
