Concept Instruction

Digital access means having the devices, internet connection, and tools needed to participate in online activities. Not everyone has the same level of access, which creates what is known as the digital divide.

The digital divide is the gap between people who have reliable digital access and those who do not. This gap can be caused by economic barriers (cost of devices or internet), geographic barriers (limited service in rural areas), or ability-based barriers (lack of accessible technology for people with disabilities).

Inclusive digital participation means making sure everyone can join, contribute, and feel welcome in digital spaces. This includes designing platforms that are accessible, respecting different digital experiences, and supporting people who face access challenges.

🧠Think: Who might be excluded from an online activity if it requires fast internet, expensive devices, or advanced digital skills?

⚠️Common mistake: Assuming everyone has the same access to technology or the same comfort level using digital tools.
