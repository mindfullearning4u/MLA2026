Worked Example

Scenario:

Two students, Maya and Jordan, are assigned an online research project. Maya has a laptop at home and high-speed internet. She can access websites quickly, watch videos, and complete her work without interruptions.

Jordan, however, does not have internet at home. He must wait until he can use a public library computer, which limits the time he has to complete his assignment. Sometimes the computers are already in use, and he cannot finish his work on time.

Analysis:

  • Maya has full digital access, allowing her to complete tasks efficiently.
  • Jordan is experiencing the digital divide due to limited internet access.
  • This situation creates an unfair learning environment, even though both students were given the same assignment.

Conclusion:

This example shows why digital inclusion is important. Schools and communities must work to provide equal access so all students have a fair opportunity to succeed.
