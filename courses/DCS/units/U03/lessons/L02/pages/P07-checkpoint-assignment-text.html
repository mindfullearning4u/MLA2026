Lesson 2 Checkpoint - Passwords, Authentication & Account Security

This checkpoint verifies your ability to identify weak security practices and choose safer alternatives.

You must choose ONE of the three situations from the guided practice:
• A student uses "12345678" as their password because it's easy to remember.
• Someone refuses to turn on MFA because "it takes too long."
• A friend shares their password with someone they trust "just in case."

Required evidence:
• The situation you selected (must match one of the three options).
• The security risk in the situation.
• An explanation of why the choice is unsafe.
• A revised version of the situation showing a safer account security choice.
• One sentence explaining why strong passwords matter.

Accepted submission formats:
• Clear photo(s) of handwritten notebook pages
• PDF file
• Typed document

Your Teacher of Record will review this submission for completion and accuracy.

If the situation is not one of the three provided options, or if the security explanation is unclear, the checkpoint is not met and must be revised.

This checkpoint contributes to unit mastery and progression.
