Concept – Cyberbullying, Safe Communication & Data Protection

Cyberbullying includes threats, harassment, spreading rumors, sharing private information, or repeatedly sending hurtful messages. It can happen through texts, social media, games, or group chats.

Safe communication means staying respectful, avoiding arguments, and not sharing personal information with people you don’t fully trust.

Data protection is especially important during communication. Even simple details—your school, location, schedule, or personal photos—can be misused if shared with the wrong person.

If you experience or witness cyberbullying, the safest responses are:

  • Do not respond emotionally.
  • Save evidence (screenshots).
  • Block or mute the person.
  • Use reporting tools.
  • Tell a trusted adult.

🧠 Think: Why is it safer not to respond when someone is trying to provoke you online?

⚠️ Common mistake: Responding with anger, which often escalates the situation.
