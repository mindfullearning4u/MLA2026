Concept – Scams, Phishing, Malware & Safe Downloads

Scams and phishing messages are designed to trick you into giving away personal information, passwords, or money. They often look real but contain warning signs called red flags.

Phishing messages may pretend to be from a bank, school, or company. They often include:

  • urgent language (“Your account will be locked!”)
  • unexpected links or attachments
  • spelling or grammar errors
  • requests for personal information

Malware is harmful software that can steal data, track activity, or damage your device. It often spreads through unsafe downloads or fake websites.

Safe downloading means only downloading files from trusted sources and avoiding anything that seems suspicious or too good to be true.

🧠 Think: Why do scammers try to create a sense of urgency?

⚠️ Common mistake: Clicking a link just to “see what it is.”
