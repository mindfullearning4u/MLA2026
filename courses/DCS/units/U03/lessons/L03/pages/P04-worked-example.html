Worked Example

👀 Read the sample scenario and observe how phishing tricks people.

Scenario: A student receives an email saying, "Your school account will be disabled in 24 hours. Click here to verify your password." The email address looks slightly off: support@sch0ol-help.com.

Red flags:

  • Urgent threat ("disabled in 24 hours").
  • Asking for a password.
  • Suspicious email address.
  • Unexpected request.

Safe response:

  • Do NOT click the link.
  • Delete the email.
  • Report it to a trusted adult or school tech support.

Why this works:

  • It avoids giving information to scammers.
  • It prevents malware or account theft.
  • It models safe digital behavior.
