Worked Example

👀 Read the scenario and observe effective collaboration.

Scenario: A group of students is completing a shared online project. One member does not respond to messages and misses deadlines.

Ineffective collaboration:

  • Ignoring communication issues.
  • Letting frustration build without addressing the problem.

Effective collaboration:

  • Sending clear, respectful reminders.
  • Clarifying expectations and deadlines.
  • Communicating concerns early and professionally.

Why this works:

  • It promotes accountability.
  • It prevents conflict escalation.
  • It supports successful group outcomes.
