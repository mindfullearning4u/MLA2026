Worked Example

👀 Read the sample scenario and observe how digital access and inclusion are evaluated.

Scenario: A school club creates an online discussion group for members. The group requires high-speed internet and a laptop to participate in weekly video meetings. Several students cannot join because they only have a phone or have limited internet access at home.

Not inclusive:

  • Assuming all students have the same devices or internet speed.
  • Requiring technology that some members cannot access.
  • Not offering alternative ways to participate.

More inclusive digital participation:

  • Providing options such as audio-only meetings or text-based discussion threads.
  • Ensuring activities can be completed on a phone or low-bandwidth connection.
  • Asking members what access barriers they face and adjusting accordingly.

Why this works:

  • It recognizes that not everyone has the same level of digital access.
  • It removes barriers that prevent full participation.
  • It supports fairness and inclusion in digital communities.
