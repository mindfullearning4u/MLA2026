Concept - Plagiarism, Academic Integrity & Ethical Sharing

Plagiarism occurs when someone presents another person's words, ideas, or work as their own. This can happen intentionally or accidentally.

Academic integrity means completing work honestly, using original ideas, and giving proper credit when using sources. Integrity builds trust and ensures fair evaluation of learning.

Ethical sharing includes paraphrasing correctly, citing sources when required, and respecting creators' rights.

🧠 Think: Why does giving credit matter even when information is easy to find online?

⚠️ Common mistake: Copying or slightly changing words and assuming it is original work.
