Concept Instruction

Every time you post online, you make a choice about what information to share. Some information is safe to share publicly, while other information should stay private to protect your safety and identity.

Personal information includes details like your full name, address, phone number, school, passwords, location, or anything that could be used to identify or target you.

Public information includes general interests, hobbies, or content that does not reveal sensitive details about your identity or location.

Oversharing happens when someone posts too much personal information without realizing the risks. Once something is online, it can be copied, shared, or saved-even if you delete it later.

🧠Think: What information about you would be unsafe if a stranger saw it?

⚠️Common mistake: Believing that "only friends" will see your posts or that privacy settings guarantee safety.
