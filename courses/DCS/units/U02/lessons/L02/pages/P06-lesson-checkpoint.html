🎯 Lesson Checkpoint – Personal vs. Public Information
This checkpoint verifies your ability to identify unsafe sharing and make safer posting choices.

Important: You must choose ONE of the three situations from the guided practice:

  • A student posts a photo of their school ID badge.
  • Someone shares a screenshot of a group chat that includes full names and phone numbers.
  • A friend posts a selfie in front of their house with the street sign visible.

Checkpoint Submission Requirements (Required):

  • The situation you selected (must match one of the three options).
  • The personal information that was shared.
  • An explanation of why sharing this information is unsafe.
  • A revised version of the situation showing a safer posting choice.
  • One sentence explaining why oversharing is risky.

If the situation is not one of the three provided options, or if the safety explanation is unclear, the checkpoint is not met and must be revised.

📓 Submit your notebook evidence using the activity titled “Lesson 2 Checkpoint – Personal vs. Public Information” that follows this lesson.
