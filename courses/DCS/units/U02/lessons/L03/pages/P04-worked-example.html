Worked Example

👀 Read the sample scenario and observe how tone and clarity affect communication.

Scenario: A student messages a classmate: “You didn’t do your part. Fix it.”

Problems with this message:

  • The tone sounds demanding and accusatory.
  • It does not explain what needs to be fixed.
  • It may escalate conflict instead of solving the issue.

More respectful and clear version:

  • “Hey, I noticed the last section of our project is still unfinished. Could you take a look when you have time?”
  • “Let me know if you want help or if we should divide the tasks differently.”

Why this works:

  • The tone is respectful and non-accusatory.
  • The message explains the issue clearly.
  • It invites collaboration instead of conflict.
