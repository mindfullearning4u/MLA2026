💡Worked Example

💡Read the sample scenario and observe how online identity is formed.

Scenario: A student has a public profile where most posts are complaints about school, arguments in comments, and jokes that put others down. In person, they say they are "just joking" and "not really like that."

How others might see this online identity:

  • Negative or constantly unhappy.
  • Quick to argue or start drama.
  • Disrespectful toward others.

What the student says they intend:

  • They think they are being funny or "real."
  • They do not realize how often they post negative content.

More responsible identity choices:

  • Balancing humor with positive or neutral posts.
  • Avoiding posts that insult or embarrass others.
  • Sharing interests, achievements, or supportive comments.

✅Why this works:

  • It recognizes that others only see the behavior they post, not their private intentions.
  • It shows how small choices add up to a larger identity.
  • It models how to shift toward a more positive online identity.
