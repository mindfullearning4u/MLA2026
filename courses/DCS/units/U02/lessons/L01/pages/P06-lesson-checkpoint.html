🎯 Lesson Checkpoint – Understanding Online Identity
This checkpoint verifies your ability to describe how online identity is formed and how others may perceive it.

Important: You must choose ONE of the three situations from the guided practice:

  • A profile mostly shows funny memes, but many of them insult certain groups or people.
  • A student rarely posts, but when they do, it is always about being “bored” or “hating everything.”
  • Someone comments supportive messages on friends’ posts but argues aggressively with strangers.

Checkpoint Submission Requirements (Required):

  • The situation you selected (must match one of the three options).
  • A description of the online identity this person appears to have.
  • An explanation of how others might perceive this person based on their online behavior.
  • One suggested change that would move their online identity in a more positive direction.
  • One sentence explaining why it matters how your online identity is perceived.

If the situation is not one of the three provided options, or if the identity description and perception are unclear, the checkpoint is not met and must be revised.
