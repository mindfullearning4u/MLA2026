Concept Instruction

Your online identity is the version of you that others see on the internet. It is shaped by your profile information, photos, posts, comments, likes, and the way you interact with others. Even if you do not post often, the few things you do share still send a message about who you are.

People form opinions based on what they see, not always on what you meant. This is called perception. Your intent ("what you meant") and their perception ("what they see") are not always the same.

Over time, your choices create patterns. These patterns build a picture of your interests, values, and behavior. A responsible digital citizen thinks about how their online identity matches who they truly want to be.

🧠Think: If someone only saw your online activity, what would they assume about you?

⚠️Common mistake: Believing that "small" posts or comments do not affect how others see you.
